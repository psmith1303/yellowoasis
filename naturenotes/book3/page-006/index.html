From where the track meets the secondary road we turned left as far as East Croachy & on the first

After leaving the Dipper the road passes an unnamed lock surrounded by pines & Rodedendrons & here in broad daylight not more than 25ft from us was a lovely L.E.Owl sitting on an open fence post the ear tufts showed up very clearly. As I opened a door it flew on long spread wings into the pines.

R.B.Mergery & young

From where the track meets the secondary road we turned left as far as East Croachy & on the first small pond on the right we were very delighted to see a pair of Slavonian Grebes in full sunlight they looked beautiful, also on this pond was a Little grebe. Water lilies & M. Marigold were blooming making a nice picture. Forward on to Loch Ruthven where we stopped for tea & watched Common & B.H Gulls. As we started off again we saw our first Corvus Cornix on a fence post, more young Lapwings were ringed here. Forward on to Loch Dun Teilchaig & Loch Ashie & here were were further rewarded with 2 pairs of S.Grebes & a nest of 2 eggs & another S.Eared Owl & further Lapwing young to ring. Quite a few Hares & rabbits were about & another L.E.Owl in flight.
