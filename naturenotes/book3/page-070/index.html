Sunday 16th Feb AKW. BF. GE.

Weather very poor & overcast, not cold not very much about at all. After a brief spell, looking around Badger sett & having a fire under a heavy overhand of Yew we left the area, not before we noted the animals had been digging much more & seemed to be getting ready for the coming young. The usual more common birds were seen here & plant life noticed was the arum, which had come up quite a bit & opened into leaf.

We left the area & carried onto Fingringhoe wick, where we went straight down to the hide. The weather had got worse & cold. As soon as we opened the hide shutters we saw a pair of Shelducks & managed to get a picture of the ♀ on the mud, the ♂ flew off quickly & as soon as we photographed the ? she flew off to. The were quite a few hundred more shelducks up along the creek further on. We also saw O. Catcher, Dunlin, Red Shank & Grey Cormorants & Grey Plover & a flight of Brents when we left also various gulls. G.B.B.C. Gull. BH. & Herring.
