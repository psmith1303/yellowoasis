Nature Notes 1960 -- page-111

…422 ft high & they abound with many Puffins Guillemots, Razorbills, Shag, Fulmar & Kittiwakes. Rock Dove. Wheatear, the plants on the cliffs are very bright as was Red Campion. Ragged Robin. Rose root. Thrift & Thyme, nearby the whole area over the cliffs is covered with spring squill a nice bell shaped blue flower. Photography of the puffins was quite good being about 40 feet & the nesting Fulmars. A nearby loch off the road held two Red throated Divers & one more flew in, making four in all. The weather on the cliff was blowing a gale & with rain. A noticeable thing about the Razorbills was the Lemon Yellow inside the mouth. Both this bird & the puffing & Fulmar we were able to approach within ten feet. Remains of Puffins & Razorbill (heads beak) were found on the cliff tops, probably the work of Peregrine as we are told they are along this coast. G.B.B. Gulls were flying around & so was the Shags, which nested in one place below us. Puffins came in with fish…
