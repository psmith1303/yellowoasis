Nature Notes 1960 -- page-023

Previous page Next page

A late staying visitor was seen as a Redwing flitted about in a Hedgerow with Blackbirds & thrushes.

Only light drumming was heard during the morning & the “chuck” of the G.S.W.

Plant life is coming on well & Primroses & Wood Anemone was in bloom & Dogs Mercury. Cuckoo pint is getting on very well and abundant.

Two views were had of a Kestrel once over the wood & again down by the river, near old nesting site. A Heron was heard near the wood.

Other birds seen were wren, dunnock. Song thrush, Pigeon, Moorhen, Jackdaw, Jay, Magpie, Rook & Crow & ♀ Pheasant. Herring & B.H.Gulls flying over & Mallards.

One interesting sight was a dead Robin which appeared to have entangled itself in air & with its foot caught, it had hung in this position it was decapitated. A photo was taken.
