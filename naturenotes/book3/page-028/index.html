BF & AKW

8.12.62

Donyland Area & Roman river from Mill to Manwood

Early weather windy & wet, turning to very sunny windy & cold.

Many Pigeons about & Fieldfares & Redwings over the whole area. On the valley meadows on a line pole a Green Woodpecker was changing & we were pleased to see a large party of c.a. 50 Chaffinches most of which were ♂ ♂ & very bright, these were feeding with Starlings. Photographed the old & new bridges below the Whalebone inn. Three Snipe were flushed from the Phragmites reed & a Little Grebe was seen on the river. A Heron flew up & on toward Manwood. In the background flooded meadow were a pair of Mallard & about 4 Teal. & one feeding Redshank. This species was seen twice more flying quite high, as when earlier did a flight of Widgeon. Tree sparrow’s were along the Blackthorn hedges & thrushes blackbirds Dunnock & Wren & Robin, two more Little Grebes were watched further along the river toward Manwood very close.
