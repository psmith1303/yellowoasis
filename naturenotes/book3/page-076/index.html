8.3.64 AKW. GE. BF.

A very bright, but very cold morning, we parked as usual & caught up with GE, who had arrived earlier & had been listening to the G.S.Woodpeckers drumming in Charlottes Grove having quite good views as the bird worked up one part & then another, making quite a different sound.

We had breakfast early & a nice fire in the “wikkiup”. BF’s trap was empty, as expected because the weather was very cold the previous evening. We found a young Rabbit which was dead, the head was eaten & it appeared to have the disease Myxamotosis.

The Green Woodpecker was calling as we made our way to the bridge by Col Mangles seeing Lapwings in the fields both sides. We continued to south of Fridaywood & right through to the M.C.E. end, we noticed Hazel catkins were well out in sheltered spots, as was plants of Wild Arum nearly everywhere.
