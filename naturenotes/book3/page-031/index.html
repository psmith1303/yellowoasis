Nature Notes 1960 -- page-031

Kestrel seen over marshy area.

Recent burrows in Friday Wood by rabbits.

…through the wood & here we saw 2 or more Siskins, we back tracked these to the stand of Elders & here were quite a party of about 25 Siskins feeding on the cones & 2 or 3 Goldfinches with them. Pigeons where were very thick 200+. Further along we saw a nice little party of Long Tailed tits. We followed these to the riverside & disturbed a L.S. Woodpecker which flew off up into the wood. We followed up but did not see it any more. But a G.S.W. flushed by quietly, the L.S.W. called out rather like a Blackbirds alarm note. Near the bottom of Friday wood I saw a Snipe which was very tatty indeed.

Coming out of the Friday wood near Col Margles we started a Green Woodpecker from the ground which flew off out of sight. We carried on alongside Park farm & near the bridge on to the pipes which we crossed over & once again along the marshy ground & back to the car. Jays were seen & Robin. Dunnock. ♂ Chaffinch. Blue tit Starling. Stock Dove. House Sparrows. Meadow pipit. Carrion Crow.
