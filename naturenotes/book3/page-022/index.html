Sunday 8.4.62

GE. AKW. ND. BF.

Park farm area & Wood, Windy, cool & sunny.

Very good views of ♂ Lesser spotted Woodpecker & ♂ G. Spotted Woodpecker, in the wood & later a Green Woodpecker flying into woods, this bird was calling loudly most of the morning. Marsh tits were in the wood, as were Blue, Great & long tailed tits & a tree Creeper. No Nuthatches today, but a lovely view was had of a Barn owl which flew into a holly tree & was quickly mobbed by a Missel Thrush.

George, Godfrey & I left Brian & Dickey & walked down to the river, where I found a Song thrushs’ nest with two eggs which were gone, when we came back 20 minutes later, one broken egg found nearby, probably a mammal.

Our first 62 Chiffchaff was heard & seen near the wood, very pleasing. In a long hedgerow over the river we spotted a Squirrel which held a cone in its mouth, we watched it climb & eat the cone seeds & collected the cone centre when the squirrel dropped it. Excellent views in lovely sunlight of this nice animal.

A further great & very pleasing sight awaited us when we spotted our first ‘61 Sand Martins. There were 7+ birds flying over a flooded area, catching what seemed like mosquitoes. contd.
