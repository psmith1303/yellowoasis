22.3.64 BF. GE. AKW.

Down to the Roman River valley which is still well flooded & the river quite high. There were approximately 5,000 B.H.Gulls swimming & settled on the flooded rea. We were well pleased to see a ♂ Shoveller & a party of Teal & a few Mallard. Numbers of Swans were flying around. Skylarks were singing well & Dunnocks. The Green Woodpeckers were calling well, but not to been seen at close quarter. In fact three birds flew of the same old Elm tree, probably courtship.

We had breakfast on Rock farm bridge whilst watching the Gulls. Chickweed & Gorse was in bloom.

We left the area to go over to D. Lords Wildfowl collection & G.E. photographed a few of the specimens. B.F. & I & D Lord wandered about his land & saw approx 100 Fieldfare & a Yellowhammer & Bullfinches.
