Nature Notes 1960 -- page-091

…time. We made our way accross to Rock farm bridge & in the thicket near there located a nice Chaffinchs nest empty as yet. The nest by the dyke over near the manor was a Dunnocks & it had three broken eggs.

To many gun happy men in these parts these days, as we were approached by Cypriot type greasers all with 12 bores & seemingly no experience with them. Later two young men with spears net & knives after a duck with a nest in the dyke.

We had coffee with “Ma” at rock farm & she gave us some nice new sacking for hides. Swallow on were. We spoke to Col Mangles & he is going to see if we can get permit of authority to question would be gunners & trespassers & keep an eye on the territory.

Arum now has the spathes up & many more plants are blooming.

On to Berechurch hall & check out on GSW hole in chestnuts also to see that Badgers have been very active, probably young now. We left for home from here. Cuckoo seen & heard also chiffchaffs & Bullfinches.
