Sunday 5th Jan 1964 : B.F. G.E. AKW.

Down to Roman River by dawn which was late due to mist & overcast conditions. At about 7.30am flocks of Starlings let a roost which we have located over in land owned by Platt. How many birds in this roost? There were 3 or 4 waves coming over in our direction towards the north or Colchester way & there could well have been 4 to 5,000 in each wave. We inspected the roost area mainly in thick blackthorn & Sloe thickets, the amount of droppings did not seem very much for so many birds - this may be due to the roost being quite new - to our knowledge the first time this winter of 1963⁄4. We could hear quite a noise from this area before each flock took off. We made a clearing in readiness for roost work at night make two or three pathways.
