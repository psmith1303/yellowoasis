Sunday 5th April ‘64 AKW. BF. GE.

At last a sunny but windy day & early morning frost which soon cleared. Down to the R.River valley. G.E. saw a Green Woodpecker quite close, also a Woodcock which appeared injured, but avoiding capture. B.F. & I were a bit late & we helped G.E. get his car out of a bogged down situation, this he got into after chasing a fox into the old ammunition shelters. In the wood we were pleased to see the Wood Anemone in bloom also Dogs Mercury & Lesser Celandine. Arum is coming up fast. We breakfasted on the pipes seeing plenty of Starlings in the field adjacent. We carried onto the Rock farm bridge & just over this we found a perfect Badger spoor. B.F. made a nice plaster cast. I was pleased to photograph a nice clump of Coltsfoot nicely out in flower. Over to the Willows & poplar swamp we saw Mallard & old blue tits nest prospect. BF saw a fox near Rock farm bridge.
