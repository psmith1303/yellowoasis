Nature Notes 1960 -- page-053

Previous page Next page

…to see more Bullfinches. Back along the river to the copse, nothing eaten off the goose body. A pair of swans were on the river & the cob had a large knob on it bright orange bill. Another Moorhen seen nearby. Chaffinches were singing spring notes. Further on to the Fir stand we saw a small party of L.T. tits & then a party of c.a. 6 Bewicks flew over on towards the resavoir. A wren was seen in the fir stand & Blue tit. Jays & Magpies became more noisy by now, but the weather remained very dull, but not cold. We came back through Charlottes grove & had very good views of a G.S. Woodpecker. B.F. saw a G. Woodpecker fly from the fir stand.
