Nature Notes 1960 -- page-065

…saving, so we had it. Much evidence of a lot of birds falling prey to supposedly a fox. A new excavation, by an old earth near Ball farm, has been recently dug out by the animal & we hope this will be in use later on. B.F. saw a Barn Owl near this area during the previous Thursday night & we decided to investigate any holes in trees, this we did and found a Barn Owl pellet (quite large) in an old Elm tree. but unfortunately no Barn Owl. We think this may be in the old barns at Ball farm (we hope).

We saw quite a few birds this morning including a Green Woodpecker, Jay. Magpie. C.Crows. Jackdaws. Rook, Redwing, Fieldfare. Missel thrush, Robin, Bullfinch. Heron (perching in the usual tree by the Manor). A flock of Woodpigeons. 3 or 4 Mute swans flying over. Dunnock, Starlings, Blue & Great tit. The season has been reasonably mild so far & we saw shoots of Wild arum up 4” & eaten off by unknown mouths.

After this last walk, we got G.E. from hide & packed up to make our way home around 12.30.
