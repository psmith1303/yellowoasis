Nature Notes 1960 -- page-067

…on the outskirts of the wood we saw a small party of L.T. tits & tried to photograph them, to no avail. In the wood near Col Mangles, we photographed quite a nice little moth & then let it go, to be identified later.

Back to GE, who reported photographing a ♀ Blackbird on a post near the hide. We packed up at the hide as a Heron flew toward its perching tree. GE reported too that he had seen nearby & also had heard foxes making the noise as they did the previous week.

We all went back through Charlottes grove. In here many shoots of Bluebells were coming up & I was surprised to see Dogs Mercury in bloom so early.

Other birds we saw were Great tit, Jays Rooks. Crows & various gulls in flight. A few Bullfinches in Friday wood & Chaffinches & Dunnock. We caught a moth in Charlottes grove of the ‘Carpet’ variety (to be identified) as a Spring Usher.
