Nature Notes 1960 -- page-084

Previous page Next page

…round the old pond & saw about a dozen Snipe fly up, also some teal & Mallard & Widgeon. Moorhens were courting on the flooded ground. A few Pigeon were flying around & Jays, Magpies & Crows as well as the Rooks at the Manor rookery.

Pied Wagtails were active in several places. Many more Snipe flew up as we crossed the dyke onto the land below the Manor here on the dyke water a ♀ Mallard acted very strange as though it had a nest around, we were close to it when it dived underwater & disappeared completely. Back over Rock farm bridge & pipes & along by Col Mangles meadow to photograph the Alder catkins & here to our great delight we saw a Kingfisher which flew off over the fir stand. We went back through Charlottes grove & then to the cars.

1 Heron was also seen over the Manor.
