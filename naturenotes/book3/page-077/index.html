Nature Notes 1960 -- page-077

Previous page Next page

…pleased to see a Red Squirrel & managed a photograph of it near here we inspected a nest which was in the course of being built probably that of a Missel thrush. Plenty of Jays in this part & Jackdaws. We left this part & walked back to Charlottes Grove & here we were pleased to see a Grey Squirrel on the ground, which soon made off to the safety of a tall poplar tree. We collected the trap & GE photographed some Fungi & from near that place I could see the Catkins on the Alders near Col Mangles were showing a nice colour red.

We left this area with the cars & went on to the Badger ‘setts’, here to photograph Wild arum & Badger dung pits & tracks & scratchings on the trees. Earlier down by Charlottes Grove & the Ford we saw a very dark Red squirrel which we believe we have seen before. We had a more enjoyable morning & with more success than of late.
