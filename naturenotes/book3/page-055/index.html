Nature Notes 1960 -- page-055

…from the river near the pipes. We saw as usual Magpies Rooks, Jays, Dunnock & Bullfinches, later we saw the Heron a couple of times & las time it perched on the favourite tree near the Manor. A pair or two Widgeon flew over towards the Resavoir. It was nice to see a tree creeper during breakfast. The Green Woodpecker was heard. Over in the fir stand we saw a Marsh tit & not much else. Nothing on the river. The weather by now was very mild & very overcast and slightly misty. G.E. was in the hide & Magpies, Jay & Robin & Blackbird ♀ & Great tit came near but not to be photographed. On our way back we saw Chaffinches & Bullfinches & Jackdaws. Crows giving spring call & also Chaffinch singing. Blue bells in Charlottes Grove were up nearly 3 inches - to mild though in this wood its very sheltered. 2 Partridges were seen up near the old magazine.
