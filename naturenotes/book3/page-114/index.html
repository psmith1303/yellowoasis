…be a Sparrowhawk. Coming through Bowes moor we saw a nice Short eared Owl fly up from the road edge.

Back on the road to Dunnet head we saw two Red throated Divers flying & they called very much like a Mallard. In this area there was a large amount of Cotton Grass & Lousewort. The moor parts were dug out in many places & the “peats” were all stacked & drying out.

Here in Battan forest at this time of 10.20pm it is very light & we are parked beneath giant Douglas first some 200 ft high & easily 9 1⁄2 ft approx in circumference & magnificent they look about 23 in all in this clearing, some have a yellowish colour caused by a lichen on the bark, making them look even nicer in the sun.
