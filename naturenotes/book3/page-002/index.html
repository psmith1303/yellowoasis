Saturday May 1960

Our arrival at Aviemore was timed at 5.30 from there G.E. & Son & I made our way to the Coylumbridge are were Mr Snoxell from Col was staying. After establishing contact & having tea we made a quick survey of the wooded area immediately around us. There were many interesting plants everywhere, Oystercatchers were very common along the Larig Gru river, which is a beautiful fast clear cold running stream (where, for the rest of our stay we used for washing & drinking).

At about 10.30pm that evening we saw our first Woodcock roding, first it gives it whistling half trill then grunts, doing this a few times as it circles the Birch tree tops. Mr Snoxell took us further on to where the Capercailie roosted, we heard them crashing out of the trees but the only thing we saw of them was more or less a flash of brown, not very satisfactory view.

The most fascinating thing about the Highland at this time of the year, is the time at which the light continues which is well after midnight, up to the hour we were collecting Bordered white moth, which were very plentiful around the pines. We did get two 1 specimens of Tawny burred anglemoth.
