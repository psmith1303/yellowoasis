…then they moved off & joined another group of maybe 50+. Some stags had fine heads. Downwards we flushed another Red Grouse. The day turned very warm & sunny. We moved off through the rough little road which eventually arrived at Killdonan & then on to Dounreay & then to the most northerly tip in Scotland - Dunnet Head. Along the track before our destination we saw many Wheatears young & parents. Also some time watching the lovely Ring Ouzel which had a young one nearby. Rabbits were very plentiful & we saw 2 very dark hares (I’ll mention here a young stoat or Weasel which we passed in the car, playing by the roadside on the road from Dalwhinnie to Laggan). Sandpipers & Oyster Catchers were calling loud & nearing Killdonan. We saw a hedgehog ambling by the roadside we photographed this & then carried on. Many peat cuttings we seen on all this journey.
