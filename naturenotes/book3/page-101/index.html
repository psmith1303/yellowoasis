…all along the cliffs photographing the Guillemots, Razorbills & Puffins & Shags all of which were very nice birds. During this trip we saw a fox on the cliff bottom. Fulmars & Kittiwakes were seen up at Durlston head near the Tilly whin caves. Allen took us in his boat & showed great care & knowledge of the area & the coastline.

We were pleased to see Stonechats & a Wheatear, also Rock Dove & near the last days of our stay the great delight of seeing a ♂ Kestrel followed by a Peregrine which sailed below us & along the cliff face near St Albans head was a fitting ending enough, but although we saw the Peregrine no more the Kestrel gave us some excellent views hovering or motionless on the air current first by the cliff face.

Rock Pipits were common enough. On a small pond was a Moorhen with young. As regards the insect life…
