Nature Notes 1960 -- page-029

Previous page Next page

Several Curlew were in a meadow with Rooks & crows & Magpies & Starlings from this area through the Phragmites bed to the edge of the woods cutting through a swamp onto the Mersea Road at Manwood bridge. Here B.F. saw a Woodcock & then we saw it when we flushed it again. Ice was covered by water in the swamp & B/F got a bootful whilst emptying this we heard a terrific scream of a Water rail. On to Bell farm & plenty more Redwings & fieldfares & superb views of Goldfinches. Down by the river near Bell farm a pair of Mallard flew up & looked beautiful in the sunlight. On the river yet another two Little Grebes & two Swans.

Moorhens were seen earlier at the Fingringhoe bridge. A supposed moorhen was seen in Donyland woods very earlier & this could easily have been the Water rail we heard later, as it was near this area we heard the bird.
