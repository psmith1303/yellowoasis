Nature Notes 1960 -- page-109

The sweet smelling Wood Vetch looked very nice as did Wintergreen Chickweed. There were Bullfinches Chaffinches & Siskin feeding on Hornbeam seeds. Wrens were singing well. A wood mouse as seen here, among the numerous wood sorrel leaves. Common Ladies Mantle was numerous as was Water Avens & Yellow Pimpernel. From Battan to Lairig & on to Beinn Mhealaich (1940 ft) in Sutherland where a lovely valley continues for many miles, little habitation & quite wild. We climbed the hill mentioned & saw a ♀ dash of into the stunted lichen covered birch trees. We soon flushed a Red Grouse & then saw a Ring Ouzel. The mountain terrain was typically wet & boggy as well sphagnum moss & Butterwort, Lousewort. We were pleased to find Sundew & later Lesser twayblade. Two Lizards were seen camouflaged well against the dark ground. We found another frog & a pool full of spawn & tadpoles. We watch a herd of 17 deer some time,…
