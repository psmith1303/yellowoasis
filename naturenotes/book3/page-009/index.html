…in abundance & collected specimens. As the hard going gave way to sparce rocks here & there & smooth areas of grass & Saxifrage we came across quite a few specimens of Black mountain Moth of which we took 13 specimens. One other plant which now showed up at this height approx 3,300 was Mossy campion, at the top of Lurcher crag the land fell away to about 3000 ft & on this slope we photographed & collected Trailing Azalea. The most triumphant moment came next when we were crossing a plateau, & and I had left G.E. & Mr Snoxell & GE Jnr I went to look over the edge into the Larig gru. We had been hearing bird notes & couldn’t place them when all of a sudden I heard them very close & looking up near the edge of the mountain spotted a ♂ & ♀ Dotterel & what magnificent birds these were of lovely back stripes & beautiful chestnut breast, the most distinctive…
