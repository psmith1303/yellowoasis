Sunday 29th /12/63 Roman River. B.F. AKW

Whilst walking past the S/E corner of Charlottes Grove we hear a loud rushing sound & looked up to see at least a 1,000 Starlings, which we assumed came from a roost over in the thicket near platts ground. It was still very dull as dawn was approaching & the fog did not help it was very damp.

Redwings & Fieldfares were scattered in this flock. Down by the river at the copse a Snipe flew off & we left a Goose body for the birds in front of the hide. Not many birds to be seen in this weather. A few Bullfinches were seen by the Mersea Rd near Manwood hill & we crossed the road here into Donyland. Rooks were flying near hear. We followed the water course through the wood out on the south side by the River where the tide was coming up. We saw a Moorhen in a tree here & not many other birds until we got to the road…
