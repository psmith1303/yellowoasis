Sunday 12th April 1964 GE. BF. AKW.

Following up from the previous afternoon when I climbed a tree where a Kestrel frequented, I showed BF & GE where it was over the Sheepen fields Col. From here we went down to Charlottes grove. After a very fine week of warm & sunny weather, it altered quickly to make this day cloudy & showery. Jays were seen & Bullfinches & a G.S.W. was drumming. Plenty of Wood Anemone were out in the wood & ground ivy.

We had our breakfast in the lee of the hedge by the pipes watching the Carrion Crows & Rooks flying around also seeing 1 House Martin over the swampy ground.

We went over to the Willow & Poplar stand where the Carrion Crow of the previous year nested & found renovations had been done to it for this years nesting we hope. We looked in a hollow tree in the Starling roost but found nothing. In these & adjacent wood were nice clumps of Primroses in the shadow of some robust Sallow bushes in full bloom.
