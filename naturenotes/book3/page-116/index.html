Nature Notes 1960 -- page-116

…off Wrens which “exploded” & one came & settled on my front which I caught & examined & let go. Directly this happened we saw a Red Breasted Merganser fly off from the river, very nice & low.

Oystercatcher were on the shingle banks & one crept away & we found the nest of 3 eggs. We saw Crested tits & a Coal tit. Before we got back to the car & prepared for our journey home. There were several Pearl Bordered Frittilaries on the wing & Bordered White moths. 2 other female Roe deer were seen & both headed toward the hills. Going back to Battan forest much Water Avens was in bloom & Spearwort. What appeared to be young trout were plentiful in the river.
