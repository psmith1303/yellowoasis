27.1.63

Leewick. AKW. B.F. G.E.

A clear day & mild after bad weather of the week, thaw set in, but huge blocks of ice as big as a lorry, & in the sea causing a promblem to the ships as we saw one get stuck & brought to a standstill.

There was a good number of Brents about 2-300! & gulls of the species Black headed. Herring. G.B.B. & some not identified. Waders were quite numerous. Curlews Redshanks, Turnstone. Dunlin. Grey Plover. Bar tailed Godwits. Sanderling. Oyster catcher’s. Pipits were among these & nearby were Shelducks, Eiders ♀♀ & ♂♂ imature. & Golden Eye. A Great Crested Grebe was seen swimming in the sea. A G.B.B.Gull kept harassing the Eiders to no effect except the ducks dived under. A flight of Mallard were seen toward Brightlingsea. Reed Bunting were seen on the Suedra bushes & Linnets.
