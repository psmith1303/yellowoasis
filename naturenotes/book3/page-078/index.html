15.3.64 GE. AKW. BF.

On this positively awful cold wet day we visited the Roman River valley at about 7am. Parking the car by the disused ammo-dump we saw two Bob white Quail which quickly disappeared. We then walked down to the river, hearing a Green Woodpecker & surprisingly a fair amount of bird song. A dark or wet pheasant was by the river & a flock of Starling’s. The river was the highest I’ve ever seen it, in some parts the banks were overflowed & this caused the low lying land to be flooded to a depth up to 2ft, from Abberton Manor to Manwood bridge & on the South side of the river. Many B.H.Gulls were flying over this & quite a few Mallard & a couple of Teal, a snipe was seen later as was a Woodcock near the Quails.

We walked up to the starling roost area & all flooded was what we found…
