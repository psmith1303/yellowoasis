June 18th 1965 Scotland. GE. AKW.

We made our first point of interest to see around Ben Lawers. Our journey this far was good & in warm weather & fairly bright sun. a stop was made to photograph some flowers of Meadow Cranesbill which were plentiful.

Arrived at the Ben Lawers mountain was about 2.30pm [frog seen up on mountain] & we parked & prepared to climb. A nature trail is laid out & marked for visitors to see the various flora. Which included Mountain Fern Yellow mountain Saxifrage. Alpine Ladies Mantile. Lesser Spearwort. Lousewort. Deer Grass. Tormentil. Blue Milkwort. Heath Bedstraw. Common Speedwell. White Clover, Sphagnum. Cotton Grass. Butterwort. Moss Campion. Purple Saxifrage. Raven’s flew overhead & croaked. We went to the summit 3989 ft at which a strong wind blew. GE managed to photograph Ptarmigans of which we saw 2 ♂♂ & a ♀ in the rocky outcrop at the top. We also heard the Snow Bunting & saw later lower down Wheatear & Meadow pipit.
