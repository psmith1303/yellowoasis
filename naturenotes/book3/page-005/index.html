Monday

Leaving our base at Colylumbridge we travelled to Carrbridge where we at our first stop on the bridge, we saw a ♂ & ♀. S.Flycatcher, a Orchids flowers. Redstarts. Lapwing. Oystercatcher & Common sandpiper. Over the bridge we turned immediately left and took the lovely road which eventually took us high over the mountains, stopping only on a mountain road to watch a ♂ S.E.Owl & then it dawned on us that there must be young about, a rummage among the first flushed the ♀ & then we found & ringed 3 young, this was about 2 miles from Laggan. Many stops were to be had on this track to ring Wheatears & admire glorious scenery. On this same track just before it meets the secondary road between Tarn & Brin, on the right hand side as the river parralles the track, we stopped & personally had excellent views of at 15ft range of a Dipper which was never still bobbing its head & continually blinking show off the well known nictating membrane. I saw it feeding, immersing its head wholly & parts of its body but I did not see it go under completely. The white breast…
