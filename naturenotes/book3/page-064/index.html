Nature Notes 1960 -- page-064

We got quite near & then they saw us but only briefly did they pause, & move on further a little & start to fight again. They soon realised we were near & made off quickly towards park farm, where we trailed them to. They must have been still fighting as we heard this peculiar harsh Jay like sound as the two were in combat, further on. At first sighting the Foxes, BF. saw three, nice specimens with larg bushy tails & we thought the commotion was from, a duck or some prey in mortal danger from the foxes. G.E. heard all this noise & when we told him, he said he had hear the noise earlier on, the time of the event was approx; 9.30am.

Once again leaving G.E. we went down river & by Rock farm bridge we were pleased to see a nice Trout some 10-12 inches long. Not much further on we disturbed a Tufted duck from the river & further still some 3-4 Teal & one Snipe, of which species we had flushed earlier from the marshy or flood ground. We found a dead Magpie of which only the skull was worth…
