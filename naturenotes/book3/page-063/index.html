Sunday 26th AKW. BF. GE. Roman River Valley

Arrived at dawn & first thing installed GE in the hide in the Willow stand. B.F. & I made our way to the Starling roost over on Platts ground, much droppings in evidence & birds feeding on farmyard or grain food.

After this we had breakfast & then looked around for a good photographic specimen of Jews Ear fungi but of no avail, we did notice that young shoots of primrose were coming through. The weather started off bright after dawn & then quickly turned into fog, which persisted throughout the morning, although it was quite mild.

We went back to GE. in the hide to get him out as the weather was poor, but he said he’d have another go, which he did & once more we left him & went towards the pipes, when we heard quite a din hear the pipes we hurried to the spot to have a grand view of two Foxes fighting each other, they were standing on their hind legs & had their forelegs locked together & were knashing at each other with their teeth.
