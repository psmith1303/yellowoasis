Nature Notes 1960 -- page-073

Previous page Next page

…Manor entrance as far as Layer brook. In this field were quite a few Lapwings & Starlings & Jackdaws & Rooks. We entered this field & walked along the row of Willow inspecting any interesting nesting holes. A pair of Bullfinches flew before us, & further on a Jay was busy on the grass pecking away. G.B.B. Gulls or Herrings Gulls flying overhead were quite noisy. We crossed the pipes & spent a little while sitting near the wood watching for voles or mice to no avail & then went through the wood back to the cars & homewards. We saw a Coal tit near the dyke by the Manor.
