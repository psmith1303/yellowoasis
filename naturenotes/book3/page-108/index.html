Nature Notes 1960 -- page-108

…moved out & very soon saw Hooded Crows. At the base of one pine we saw a young ‘Hoodie’ & captured it & then after failed to catch two other young. This tree was a Scots pine & contained the nest. The field had many flowers. Heath Spotted orchids. Northern Marsh. Globe flowers. Marsh Marigold. Ragged Robin. Bog Bean cotton grass. Spearwort. Lousewort. Motoring on to Inverness & to Battan Forest we saw many orchids. Wheaters, Curlew, Oyster catcher. Lapwings. B.H. Gulls & Common Gulls. Dippers were seen twice. Common Sandpiper were seen several times. Water Avens & Mountain Pansy were numerous. A Buzzard was watched at one place & went into a tree on a Hill covered with Rhododendrons, nice scene.

Battan forest was a luxuriant mixed wood with much flora. Huge 20 ft Douglas first were impressive. A pair of Buzzards were seen several times in this forest. We saw Birds nest Orchid & photographed it, also the lovely Common Wintergreen.
