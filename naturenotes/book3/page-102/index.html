…on particular beetle was very common it was black & metallic the ♂ smaller than the ♀ & the ♂ larger clinging pads on the feet than had the ♀

Butterflies were quite numerous during the warm spells of sunshine & they included, Small Blue, Common Blue, Dingy & Grizzled Skipper. Painted Lady. Red Admiral, Small tortoiseshell. Small white & Large whites.

Plant life was very good & the finding of a nice specimen of Southern Marsh orchid provided much pleasure, there was a nice patch of this species. The cliff tops were covered in plants including, Early Gentian, Yellow Vetchling, Clary, Viper’s Bugloss, Kidney vetch, Milk thistle, Pale Flax. Wild Carrot Rock Samphire, Sea Campion, Birdsfoot trefoil, Common Broomrape, Wayfaring tree’s, Hearts tongue fern. Wild Thyme, Goats beard.
