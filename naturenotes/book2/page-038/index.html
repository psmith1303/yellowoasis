6.6.59
Sand Pit By Pass Nr. Glen Ave

After being informed by S. Parker I called upon him to see his discovery of the nest of the Red-Backed Shrike. On climbing the wire fence we looked into a Willow Warbler’s nest of young. I looked around & spotted the ♂ shrike among the foliage of a small Sycamore tree, he then flew off so we looked at the nest situated quite low in the fork of a plum tree. Not very well concealed, a bulky usual structure a base twigs & grass. We never touched or looked inside the nest, which according to S.P. contained one egg.

The pair were seen later on the wire fence & I had excellent views of both.

Since this visit I have learned this nest was destroyed by boys & so ends the story of this nest. I hope the birds will nest somewhere access is impossible as this species is very scarce, in fact this is the first record for 1959.
