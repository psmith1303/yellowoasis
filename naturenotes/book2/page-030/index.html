12.5.59
W. Mersea

After reading an article in the local paper, about a Gannet at West Mersea I made an evening visit to see the bird, which was in the care of a Mr. P. Brook at W. Mersea hall.

After introducing myself he willingly showed my sister Cynthia & son David & myself the bird which was on the lawn, making a closer approach to it, it moved off to the rear of the house & here Mr Brook tried to feed it a fresh herring, only to be treated with a harsh grrk! & refused the fish. The bird was an adult in summer plumage, with the head & nape lemon yellow, black tips to primaries & outer tail feathers, the rest was white. The bill seemed large & a dirty yellowish colour. The bird appeared very owlish or hawk looking, when seen looking directly at it, eyes forward as well. Webbed feet were dark grey.

After leaving here, we paused for a while at the strood where there was about a ca. 30 Shelducks & one Heron was well as the usual Redshanks. Onward from here to the top of Manwood hill, Colchester side, I stopped to watch two Herons fly over near the rookery toward Fingringhoe & this caused the Rooks to call & caw anxiously.
