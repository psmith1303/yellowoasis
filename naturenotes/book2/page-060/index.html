19.3.61
Park Farm. &amp; Stoke by Nayland Heronry

From about 9.30am G.E., B.F. and myself went to Col. Mangles wood. The first thing we did was to climb to the top of a pine tree to investigate what we thought was a squirrels dray - no luck, it turned out to be an old magpie’s nest. After Brian had photographed me descending the tree we walked down the wood. I espied an old tree well pecked & two more holes in it, as we got closer a Great Tit went into the hole, but came out & flew off, to stay around the trees. A hole in another tree, which we had observed last week contained fresh twigs & we stepped back to watch awhile, whilst we watched the tree several birds flew around Blue tit, Tree Creeper, Nuthatches, Marsh tits & 1 Long tailed tit. A Greater Spotted Woodpecker ♀, alighted on a birch quite close in a Starling stance, which we took it to be at first & George pointed it out, it flew off. A few minutes later it must have circled round & we could hear it pecking. We moved up to near where the old well pecked tree was, then I spotted it on an oak tree we had fairly good views before it flew off again. Brian stayed by the pecked tree with his camera & tripod hoping to snap the tit going in the hole again. George & I left him to it & wandered down the side of the wood. & out down the pathway to the river. As we got on the corner, I caused George to listen & we both saw & heard our first ‘61 Chiffchaffs, so nice, probably just arrived.
