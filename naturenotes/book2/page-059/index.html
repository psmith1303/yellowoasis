Nature Notes 1959. Page 059

Previous page Next page

…Partridges flew up from nearby plouged fields. A Green Sandpiper also flew up from the dyke’s muddy edges & two more Snipe.

I collected some seaweed wrack for R. Leavett, from some posts at the channel edge, to be identified.

We left here quite soon & on my motor cycle we made our way to Park farm wood. The weather was cold & very windy, but with sunshine in patches. We continued our search in the wood for woodpeckers nests & found none. Drumming was hear & the Green Woodpecker calling. We watched Bullfinches for a while & finished our coffee, then into a thicket where a Redwing settled just above our heads, for a minute & then flew off.

We found a cow which had died in the thicket & was torn apart & rotting the head & jaw bones were apart from the body. Perhaps a fox, Badger. Rats? Death of cow unknown - maybe foot & mouth?

Wind flowers were out (Wood Anemone) & Primroses, one or two plants remain in the wood.

We search again for our traps, but we did not find them (the mammal traps I made & set in the chestnut groves had been taken). We now give them up for good, that they have been stolen.

We came out of the wood & left at about 12.30.
