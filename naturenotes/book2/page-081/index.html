31 April 1961
Havergate Island &amp; Minsmere

The much awaited visit to Havergate started at approx; 7am. A party of six including George Evans, Brian Frost, Russell Leavett, Ken Crawshaw & Mike Cant, met at the last mentioned home as a rendezvous & we set off. Ken & Mike went in one car & the rest of us went in a Vauxhall Victor which B.F. had borrowed from his works.

Through Ipswich & our first stop was at some woods near Butley. Here we saw quite a few Redstarts mostly ♂♂ & only one or two ♀♀. A Tree Pipit flew from a tree top singing merrily, as was a Robin on the roadside. The Cuckoo was calling regularly & Shelduck we flying in circuits round a nearby field. The Green Woodpecker flew across a wood calling loudly. Four Jays chased each other calling harshly & looking nice in the morning sun - a lovely fresh morning with a slight wind. Blackbirds & Woodpigeons flew around the woods & hedgerows , whilst Swifts flew & wheeled overhead & birdsong like a syphony with background music of the GSW Drumming. Pheasants & partridges were seen regularly during the day. Nightingales were singing & carried on all day.

From this roadside stop we carried on, only to stop a little further on to watch a Marsh tit, plucking fur from a dead flattened Rat on the roadside, on again, passing a Red Squirrell on the grass verge. We stopped again near a sand working, from which plenty of Sand Martins flew around & about. We all walked down the road, watching a Starling, with it head emerging…
