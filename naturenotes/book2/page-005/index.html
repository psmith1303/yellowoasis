11th January 1957
Abberton Resavoir

A visit first to see the brent Geese at Mersea, but the tide was still high and no geese feeding, however I believe I saw large parties flying in the distance. My sister C.W. saw approx.; 100 on the previous Saturday afternoon.

We left Mersea and decided to visit the resavoir, so via Peldon we went. At Wigborough we saw a few Fieldfares flying close in front of the car, & their grey-blue rumps showed up very clearly. Stopping on the first causeway before Layer Breton I got out to watch a few Teal, Pochard, Mallard and Coot. Just then I heard a curious bugling sound and looking up I saw nine swans, I believe they were Whoopers, which flew westwards.

I then walked towards the ringing caravan and I observed for my very first time - twenty three beautiful Smew, twelve were ♂♂ in adult plumage. The weather was very cold and by now it had penetrated my clothing, as the wind blew quite strongly accross the causeway. On the reed side the water was frozen over, & nothing to be seen.

We noticed many hundreds of Lapwing all afternoon and as we made our way home.
