…of the bus. A Stoat dated accross as well as when we were coming home.

Nigel Cumming was collecting Coleoptera & had an excellent day with about 30 species. I collected one or two nice specimens for him, from decayed trees.

The weather from the time we cleared Colchester until we left Royston Heath was excellent for an April day. The sun shone continuously & it was warm. - enough to entice quite a variety of bees out for pollen.

After making one or two field sketches of the anemones I took David around the woodland & saw an old Badger earth, then before we left I collected some fresh looking specimens of anemone for painting.*

Every one had a very enjoyable time & coming home nearing town at Stanway two Swifts were seen. We arrived home at 6.30pm.

P.S. About four Wheatear’s were noted together from the bus.

[*In separate large guard book ledger, a small watercolour 10cms x 16.2cms titled “Pasque Flower” (Anemone Pulsatilla). Royston Heath. Herts. 26.4.59.]
