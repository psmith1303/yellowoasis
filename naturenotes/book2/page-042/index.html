18th July - 1st Aug. 1959
Hampshire & Dorset

The first day was spent at the resort of High-cliffe. Here I had a look round & watched two Adders. I then came accross some very nice plants of Dark Mullein. The caterpillar of the Cinnabar moth were numerous on plants of fine looking Ragwort.

The second day - a Monday I met up with J. Firmin at Swannage, after seeing the wives & children comfortably we caught a bus to Worth Matravers. Here we walked down a long track to the Winspit, over some very nice flower slopes of Chalk. There were many lovely Chalk Hill blue butterflies everywhere & a few Common blues, as well as the Brown Argus. A few Marbled White were seen & small Skippers. Only one High brown Frittilary was seen on this occasion, but the Grayling was common & a good strain. We walked to the sea cliffs & here we saw Rock samphire in profusion, with a few Herrings gulls & Cormorants perched about on the Cliff rocks. The wind was blowing strong here & we turned to come away, looking up we saw a nice family party of Wheatears. Not a ♂ to be seen in adult plumage.

There were many chalk plants as follows-: Clustered Bell flower (going over). Field Gentian, Yellowort, Dwarf Carline thistle, Ploughman Spikenard, not to mention the more common species. I took some roots of the Hartstongue Fern, which was growing in a tree sheltered spot.
