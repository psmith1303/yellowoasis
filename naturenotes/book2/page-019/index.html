30.3.59
39 East Stockwell St.
The larva of the Lappet moth which Joe Firmin has had sleeved on Hawthorn at his house, are in a container on sprigs of Hawthorn but still seem a bit sleepy, as they are not eating very much yet. All the larva look very healthy, mostly all sitting along the twigs in good camouflage.
5.4.49 Walton - Naze
With R.L. & C.W. visited the naze for the morning to see migrants. We were quite lucky, for on arrival at 8.45, we had not gone far along the cliff top when in quick succession we all saw a Blad Redstart & a ♀ Wheatear, which flew out of sight after flushing them a couple of times. Carrying on further we decided to vere to the gorse covered field. We were lucky again for we had very good views of a [blank]. We carried on accross the field, pausing to look at numerous Goldfinches, Dunnocks, Skylarks & Linnets. Two Redwing were seen twice as they flew from one spot to another. One Wren was seen & heard. Blackbirds were numerous. After getting through the field we carried on along to the new sea-wall, here we saw one or two Meadow pipits. From the sea wall we disturbed a party of Redshanks and Ringed Plover. The tide was high and the saltings were filled up. Way accross these salting were about three Shelducks which flew off.
