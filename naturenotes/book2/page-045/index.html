3.8.59
R. Colne &amp; Roman River. Colchester

A quick look along the R. Colne revealed some very nice plants of Arrowhead in bloom & plenty of Yellow lily. It seems as though a good breeding season has been had by many species of fish which were in countless shoals. Young Roach & many Gudgeon & Sticklebacks.

Down the Roman River its much the same for fish & quite a few sand Dabs. Its pleasing to see the flowering Rush is plentiful & widespread. I saw one Kingfisher near the stretch to Col. Mangolds. Green Woodpeckers were seen & heard very often. I felt very pleased & lucky when I saw one black form of the Elephant Hawk Larva on Willow herb. I looked for more & saw a dropping, on closer investigation I revealed a very nice green form after this I found four more of the green form. Now I hope to rear successfully.

These pupa have now pupated & I have the chrysalis on corrugated paper. These were finally reared successfully all emerged okay.

30.8.59 Abberton Res.

During the Monday evening of the 24th Aug, RL & myself visited Abberton res; We saw our very first ♂ Crossbill which showed up very red, it only stayed long enough for identification before it flew undulating across the meadows. The water was quite low & revealed quite a bit of mud. Species on here were Snipe, Purple Sandpiper, Ringed Plover & many Wagtails Herons & Pochard & Teal were also seen.
