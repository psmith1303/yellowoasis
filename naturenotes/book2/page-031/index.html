23.5.59
Hog Wood. Sussex.

After catching the 2.31am traing a Col; & linking up with Joe Firming & Brian Frost, we made an early morning trip to London, across to Waterloo & the 5.25 to Whitley in Surrey, bus to Chittingfold & taxi to Plaistow we walked to Hog Wood to do some butterfly collecting & larva hunting.

First at Chittingfold we had breakfast on the village green & here we saw a Spotted Flycatcher & one or two cuckoos ♂&♀, the ♀’s were giving their trilling whistle.

On arrival at Plaistow we walked down the road to hog wood observing along the wayside a good batch of advanced caterpillar of the small Tortoiseshell, further along a nice ♂ of Orange-tip. Along a lane on route where many sallow bushes were growing we found a large tent of Common Lackey caterpillars of which we had a few to rear. Next we saw a Wall butterfly & when in the Hog wood proper we inspected a Buckthorn bush & found one Brimstone egg, from then on we collected a good few more on the young & short Buckthorn. Eggs & larva of the orange-tips were plentiful & we had enough to rear. The Frittilary butterfly was not to plentiful but more common than the other species save the Brimstone of which ♂♂ & ♀♀ were common.
