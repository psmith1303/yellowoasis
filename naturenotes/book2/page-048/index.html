13.9.59
Walton-On-Naze

With G.E., R.L. & J.F. early morning expedition to the naze to see what migrants were about. Our arrival was approx; 7-00am & the light was fairly good, some mist about & a sharp breeze off the sea, but generally quite good & warming up quickly.

First birds seen were a prty of 7 approx; Cormorant heading round to the E.B.W.S watcher’s hut. We struck out across the field & scrub area and saw many birds of the Fringillidae, mostly Greenfinch, Goldfinch, & Linnets, the yellow bars of the “Goldies” showing up very well. The first migrant seen was a Whinchat. We toured around a bit before going to the shore & I saw one Hare, not many birds other than the usual Gulls Lapwings & many House sparrows & a flock of Starlings coming in of the sea. It wasn’t before we were well down by the new sea wall when the first Wheatears were seen & of these were saw about four. Over the new sea wall in the space between this & the old wall, were a couple of Redshanks, & one Ringed Plover.

Down on the shore we watched more Redshanks, the Sea Aster was blooming here & there among the other plants which had only the yellow centres left. One Curlew flew off in the direction of the Tower, where in a field in the foreground, was a mixed flock of Gulls with the L.B.B. Gull very prominent.
