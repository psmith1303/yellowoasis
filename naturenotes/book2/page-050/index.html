Sunday 22.1.61
Friday Wood

With Brian Frost in search of Spring Usher moth of which we had no luck in finding.

During the morning we were surprised & very pleased to see a Lesser Spotted Woodpecker & hear it drumming so early in the year. A little later on we were pleased once again to see three Nuthatches, & a Kingfisher, & quite a flock of Mallard. Coming away from the pine area of the wood, Brian spotted an Anvil, as is made by the Greater S. Woodpecker for splitting up pine cones and eating the seeds. There were in fact a pine cone already lodged quite firmly in position in the hole which formed the Anvil. The Anvil was a tree stump about 5’ from the ground (an oak stump). This was photographed on location and secured as a specimen afterwards.

9.2.61

At work & on my way to Lexden via - the back of Lexden park I saw my first Butterfly of 1961. It was a lovely and very fresh looking xx Brimstone which was fluttering among large leaved ivy & in brilliant & warm sunshine.
