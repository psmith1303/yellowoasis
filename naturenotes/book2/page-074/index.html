Nature Notes 1959. Page 074

…river & inspected an old rotten stump which Jackdaws were building in & agitated tits flying about round it were probably nesting there as well. A pair of Bullfinches flew over to a thick Blackthorn hedge, they were the second pair we had seen during the morning, the other pair were seen near the site of the Nuthatch battleground. We walked along this hedge, up to the trees with the L.S.W. nesthole in them, Brian went forward , whilst G.E. & I looked at a dead Woodpigeon lying nearby. Brian shouted out, his hand clapped over the hole, he exclaimed “There’s a ruddy Blue tit in here.” Brian got ready with camera & hoped to snap the bird as it appeared coming out of the hole, but nothing we could do would make it come out, so we left it in piece with its make calling out above us.

Time was getting on so we went back to the hide & packed up our gear. The nuthatches made one more appearance before we left the wood at about 12.30.
