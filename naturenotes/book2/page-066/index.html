Nature Notes 1959. Page 066

…in a flash & out of sight. Over to inspect the L.T. tits nest, which had nothing in it. back again into the wood & up along the outer edge. to where there are three larger Scots pines, here Brian spotted another Red squirrel, which we did take a snap of.

After a time we returned to the hide & had a sum up of the morning on the tape. Brian interrupted this when he saw another (the 3rd) Red squirrel, up again after a picture but this one to bolted out of sight, high up the pines.

Nearing the end of the recording we heard & recorded Nuthatch song, which was quite nice & clear.

We had a very successful morning & came away well pleased at about 1230.

[Fixed to page with photo corners: Black and white photograph 11cm x 8cm. Written underneath: “A.K.Woolsey & G.Evans in the Roman River valley during Sunday April 23rd 1961. Photo by B.E.Frost.]
