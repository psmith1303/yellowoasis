17th January 1958
Harwich Rd Colchester

During the afternoon, I took my son David with me whilst I was watching the single Waxwing near the new St Annes church off Harwich road.

On arrival RL & AW were already watching the bird also the Rev. Parker who peered at it through a telescope. I was informed Mr Rose of Mistley attempted photographing the bird but the light was poor and I didn’t know if he was successful.

The Waxwing is becoming less tame and frequently flies further along when we move nearer, I got right underneath it once, about 7 or 8 feet away.

Once it flew into a nearby garden and ate two or three beak-fulls of snow, this I presume to quench its thirst as any other water was frozen over.

There were still a dozen or more Redwings on the same hedge, but the berries are withering now.

Coming through the lower Castle park I noticed a Little Grebe, as I have done these last few weeks, and can be approached quite closely.
