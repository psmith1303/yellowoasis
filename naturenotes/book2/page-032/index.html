Nature Notes 1959. Page 032

…wood, Wood White not so common at all. One Burnet - companion moth. Many Speckled Yellow moths were flying around. Other common insects were small & large & green veined White, White wave moth. Several types of Diptera about & Dragon & May flies shining lovely iridescent green. One or two Wood Ant nests about, we observed the creatures carrying away a quite large caterpillar which B.F. photographed. Rather disappointing was the Spotted Orchids not in bloom, although we found several with good heads of buds all ready to bloom. Sanicle & Woodruffe were blooming in the shade of the trees which are mostly young pines with ash, oak & silver birch to foster the pines. Along the rides were many patches of Lousewort, Broom, Cuckoo flowers, Milkwort, vetches, yellow Pimpernel, as well as the other common plants.

At 3.30, the time for us to get our taxi back we just made it to Whitley Station in the Austin Princess car doing seventy in places to catch the 4.5 train to Waterloo, back accross London by cab to catch the 5.25 to Colchester, saying goodbye to J.F. & B.F. at about 6.45 it ended the lovely successful very warm sunny day.
