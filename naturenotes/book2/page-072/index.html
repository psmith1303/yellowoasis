Nature Notes 1959. Page 072

Previous page Next page

Plant life was well underway now & plenty of Bluebells & Wood Anemones out in bloom, still a few primroses, the Archangel was in bloom & see for the first time of 1961, very nice. Wood & dog violets were also plentiful & patches of Greater Stitchwort, White deadnettle & still in profusion Dog Mercury.

Many starlings were about the old holes in the ash trees & whilst watching the a Nuthatch appeared, we watched for a while & saw another nearby inspect a hole which had freshly chipped marks all round it, all of a sudden a Blue tit went to the hole & was immediately attacked by the Nuthatch, both birds clashed & descended in a fighting ball & many feather came adrift & floated by us. the birds parted & the tit flew off to a tree nearby. The other nuthatch inspected the hole again & had a little peck & flew off, calling loudly.

We decided to erect the hide nearby to watch them so off we went for our equipment & the hide, putting it up when we came back a few feet from the tree, which was an ash.

We had coffee whilst watching & did a little recording. Again the Tit & Nuthatch skirmished in the same way as before & then again after that. The Nuthatches went off & the tit entered the hole & stayed in it for over ten minutes, when the bird flew out quickly as it heard a Nuthatch alight near the hole, again a skirmish & feathers.

A greater spotted was drumming loudly to our right, so I left Brian & George to see if I could see it. On my sway towards the drumming noise, I saw several excited Blue tits, just as we had seen them in little flocks, chasing each other…
