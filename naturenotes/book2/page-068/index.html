31.3.61
Brecklands. Eriswell Suffolk

With G. Evans on my motor cycle at about 6.00am to the brecks were G.E. had not been before. Our journey there was very cold & we stopped once for a warm up & coffee.

On the road into the brecks we found a nice, dead fresh ♂ Yellowhammer which should make a nice skin. We arrived at our destination, parked the bike & disrobed our clothes. The weather was overcast with a N/W wind & quite cold. We walked along the edge of a pine cops seeing Blue & Great tits & Lapwings in a courtship flight over the heather. Further on we passed a crow trap empty. I walked into another small wood & saw a Red Squirrel feeding on the ground, quickly to disappear, when it saw me. George was still walking a little further on & saw our first two Stone Curlews of the day. There were many dead Rabbits scattered widely over the land, all seemed to have been “Myxie” victims. The hares were unaffected by this disease & we saw as many as twenty during the day, this can be said for pheasants & partridges too.

No sooner had I rejoined G.E. I spotted two birds very much like Skylarks, only much more distinctly marked & one brighter than the other, they were more stocky than Skylarks & when they flew up, they showed a short thick tail, these were the only pair of Woodlarks we saw all day.
