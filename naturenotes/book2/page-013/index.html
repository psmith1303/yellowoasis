1 February 1959
Abberton Resavoir

With Russell Leavett we went to the resavoir via Layer road. First as already mentioned on previous entry, we called on the Waxwings, where we met John Mead.

After a while here, we went on our way along layer road, down Kingsford and to Layer causeway first. There were not a great many birds on this beautiful spring-ling morning. However after glancing at Coots and G.C.Grebes, in the far distant concrete sides I could just make out a fair party of Cormorants sunning themselves. Two of these species were seen closer on the opposite side of the road. Not far from these birds was a fair size party of Smew ♂♂&♀♀. With these I saw my very first Goosander. I made out about 5+. Not extra special views at the long range, but easily recognised when they lifted their bodies clear of the water, their showing the whitish underparts. To me they were quite large birds, with largish heads and dwarfing the Smew.

As the time was getting on we moved round to the Layer Breton causeway, by this time we were joined by A.W. and his friend. Plenty of Lapwings everywhere. Before we moved a couple of Herons flew over the water and two Mute Swans. Also we had flight views of the Cormorant.

From the next causeway we observed a small party of Teal and Tufted Ducks, another Heron quite near. Cont’d
