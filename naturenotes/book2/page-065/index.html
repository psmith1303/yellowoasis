Nature Notes 1959. Page 065

We hadn’t finished with woodpeckers, as we were discussing the events, a ♀ G.S.W. settled quite close on a decaying oak tree limb & began to chip away at the bark, of which now & then its removed quite large pieces, this afforded us very good views of its plumage & we noted buffish breast colour, crimson under-tail feathers & glossy black back & head & the normal white patches. This bird came to an adjacent tree, when I gave a woodpecker call, but whether or not it was curious, I couldn’t say & when it spotted us, it made off again with its dipping flight.

Brian decided to watch the hole in the tree from which grass & sticks were showing, from the hide. I left him & went off towards the river. Halfway down the slope to the river, an oak bole is covered in ivy & onto this from the leaves at the base of the tree, sprung a very nice darkish Red squirrel, I watched it from about eight feet, I moved slightly & it also moved a little higher, stopped & scorned me with a sort of grunting noise, then up a little higher it moved, into the broad sunlight. I left it in piece & carried on to sit for a while on the ridge watching at the same time the gorse bush where we found a Long tailed tits nest. I didn’t see the birds anywhere. After a few minutes, I went back into the wood. The windflowers were out, but not in profusion as yet. A few primroses were still blooming & one or two quite early bluebells.

Nothing was in a squirrels dray which I climbed up to, as I made my way back to B.F. he reported Nuthatches & tree creepers we about, he then suggested we should try a picture of the squirrel I had seen, so off we went, we saw it again but it was down the trunk & off accross the woodland floor…
