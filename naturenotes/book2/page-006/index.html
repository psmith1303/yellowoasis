15th January 1959
Harwich Road, Colchester

Russell Leavett informed me of a single Waxwing feeding on Hawthorn berries, with company of approx.; 40 Redwings, he watched during lunch hour, & informed me when I saw him later, so we went directly to the spot, and we watched the bird from about twelve feet, being very tame.

The red secondary wing feather quill tips were very noticeable and easy to see, denoting it was probably a ♂ bird. When we first spotted it, it moved towards a Redwing which it chased off.

The colouring of this individual seemed brighter in all its colours (except for breast) than did the birds which were seen at Cambrai Road Colchester during January of 1958.

The Redwings have been in the area for the past week, the berries are numerous here and seem to their liking, & very likely will stay a while with the Waxwing. These birds are the first I’ve had good views of, and the stripe near the eye stand out most of all and first take the eye.
