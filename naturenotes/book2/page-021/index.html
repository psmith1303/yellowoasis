5.4.59.
Afternon.Abberton Resavoir.

A change in weather from the morning of warm sunshine to rather dull skies and colder winds from the N/west did not stop very good numbers of birds to bee seen at the resavoir.

From the Layer Breton causeway first I saw 5 ♂ Shoveller & two ♀♀, the males in a courtship flight display circle the swimming ♀ and then landed close to her, displaying further by swimming round her, & touching occasionally. Good numbers of Tufted Duck, Teal flying around & parties swimming. Swans in pairs as usual and G.C.Grebes in pairs were numerous, as were Coot’s. A Red Bunting was seen perching on a fence-post & another seen later at the water’s edge. Mallard & the semi-ferrule[?] ducks swam out toward the middle passing two white (escaped?) farmyard duck - all white with bright yellow/orange bill. No sawbills seen.

On the other causeway where I was very pleased to see the beautiful ♂ Yellow wagtail and as text books quote “the ♂ in early spring is as bright as the dandelions”, was perfectly true. No Wheatears seen here. The wagtail was close by a Skylark and a Red Bunting ♂ & ♀ already mentioned feeding from the water line. I watched a pair of Swans ousted off, as they flew & settled in another “cobs” territory, with wings arched well up on his back the pair soon flew away.
