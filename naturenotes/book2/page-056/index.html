About nine oclock after breakfast, I took my son David & went up to meet George Evans and Brian Frost, we then went on to Friday Wood. We left the motor-cycles in the Ramparts lane & walked across the fields. The first bird we saw, to our delight, was a ♀ Green Woodpecker which flew from ahead of us & alighted on a telegraph post. We watched it here for some time, during which it called several times & now & then preened itself. The beak opened very slightly when it called & it looked around very warily, before it flew off when George & Brian got closer. The post itself showed woodpecker work & had fair sized weather cracks in it, these no doubt contained insects, beetles & spiders which attracts woodpeckers to these poles, even to nest in. We moved on a few yars & we saw a ♂ bird fly into some wiry looking brambles & it perched on these in a typical passerine posture for a half a minute & then flew on a few more feet to another bramble, this time in typical woodpecker fashion. The bright sun shine upon this bird in flight & the yellow gold rump showed up very well.

We continued on to the fir stand & circled through the wood stopping to watch Linnets & a ♂ Bullfinch & several tits. Out of the wood & along the edge to another fir stand where we saw a Goldcrest. We stopped a while & had coffee then carried on to open ground near Col Mangles house.
