Nature Notes 1959. Page 098

…watching a tree creeper, returning to its nest which contained one young.

A Heron flew up from the dyke & made off towards the Resavoir.

We returned through the wood to the car disturbing a Green Woodpecker as we went through & also investigating a rotten tree full of holes, but none inhabited, on arriving at the car, it rained fairly heavily whilst we had coffee for about 15 minutes, after this we watched a Chaffinch & then a Blackcap, before leaving Brian.

George, Dickie, & I carried on toward the Mersea road & left the car & M/cycle & walked across the fields, to where we knew of a Green Woodpecker nesting in an oak tree.

G.E. & myself climbed up armed with a net & scope, both of which proved unsuccessful as the hole was too deep & the bird which was with-in would not leave, until as we gave it up & departed the bird flew off & when we were a little distance away the assumed ♂ bird flew in the direction of the nest. As we walked along I spotted a pair of Cream-spot tiger moths, which we collected & hope to breed from, they were in excellent condition. We had a look round further down by the river which was quite high but not much other than Linnets & a Goldfinch. We were very pleased with the morning out & returned at approx. 1230.
