13&amp;14.5.61
Park farm Wood. Dawn Chorus.

With B.F. G.E. & D an American cousin on G.E. we pitched a tent & stayed the previous evening & part of the nights in the wood. We tried nothing at dusk with the M/V bulb, but as the evening was very cool not much more than gross type moths & icneuman flies appeared.

After a few hours Sleep, we were up & out by 2.45 & the morning was cold & foggy. Bird song was very low & the first bird heard was a Cuckoo followed by the Wren & Crows & Rook, plenty of Blackbird song & Song & Missel through, Blackcaps were later & Skylark & various tits, Pigeon then Whitethroat, the chorus got a little more volume by five am but was quite disappointing as a whole. [Pencil comment: Photographed young Rook]

After breakfast we wanted down toward the river & the old willows, where a Green Woodpecker was seen & plenty of Starlings, the Marsh tit was still sitting on its eggs, or young in a hollow tree, in the hedgerow.

[Pencil comment: Wheatear seen in nearby fields big & very pink looking]

7 June 1960

During the evening GE BF & myself visited Donyland woods to inspect a tree previously found by B/F for a woodpecker’s nest, this proved false. However we did find another nest hole, very much blackened by exudance from the hole, when we got close G.E. & I heard young birds, & withdrawing back down from view watched the bird return but not enter the nest. We watched for a good 8 minutes & then the bird made off very quickly, as we didn’t want to disturb her we made off as well.
