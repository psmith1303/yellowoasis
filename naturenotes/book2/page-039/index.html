14.6.59
Tiptree & Heath areas.

An afternoon visit to survey areas for county planning officer with members of the C.N.H.S. After each group had been given an area we went to have a quick survey, my part & Cynthias was the Birch wood & area immediate, this wood will probably be cut down as the large oaks are all numbered. As well as Oaks, there are Hazel, Birch, & a few firs with much scrub undergrowth, not much Botanically we found White Bedstraw & Yellow Pimpernel & one of the Figworts.

After the survey I visited the heath area for a picnic with my family. After tea Cynthia & I looked over the heath for plants, we found as before the Milkwort & the Creeping willow. The Sing & Cross leaved Heath was in bloom, we then found two or three plants of Elegant St Johns Wort in bloom. I found one larva of the Puss Moth on the same Aspen as J.F. previously found one egg. I then showed Cynthia the seed pods of the Louseworth & here we were delighted to find two plants of the Heath spotted orchid, nearby was plants of Lesser spearwort, Tormentil & Spring Cinquefoil, this last speciies will be checked with one unidentified plan, the weather was very warm, with brilliant sun all day & an east wind. The above mentioned S. Cinquefoil was in fact another small plan of Tormentil, the unidentified one was Hoary Cinquefoil.
