…but the light was poor, again checking will be done.

No change in the G.Woodpecker hole in the willow tree, we walked the whole length of the ditch & noted many holes along the willows, some used by Starlings.

The Kestrel flew out of the elm & across the pastures to settle in the large Elms on the far side, probably the ♂ bird. A dead pigeon, caused me a wet & very muddy foot, when I crossed the ditch to make sure it was a pigeon, however this made no difference & we carried on, over the old bridge & through the wood which was quite dusky by now & cold. A whole moth was captured & identified & later released a G.E.’s home. Back to our M/cycles we cam away about 9.20.
