5.7.59
Monks Wood, Hunts.

An expedition of the New Aurelians, comprising Joe Firmin, Bill Simpson, Brian Frost, myself and Mr Firmin Snr, set out to Monks Wood, a nature conservancy wood in Huntingdonshire. After arriving a about 11.30 we went directly into action to find the Black Hairstreak butterfly, which we saw flying up on top of 22ft high Blackthorn bushes & well out of reach. We continued along the ride & Bill took a nice specimen of the Marbled White, which had a brownish underside, then a few minutes later secured a nice Silver Wash Frittilary, which had violet tinged underside, as proved the same of another captured later on. Having no success in this ride further we tried another ride, were we still had little success, only capturing a tattered type Marbled white. At this stage we stopped for lunch & enjoyed our Lagers.

After lunch we tried the other end of the wood, along the outside Joe showed us the very nice plants of the Crested Cow Wheat, which however were not at their best. The Meadow Brown & Ringlet were abundant. I was quite delighted when I saw my first White Admiral. I captured one nice Comma. By this time we walked our way back to the car & up the same ride, where we left Mr Firmin Snr…
