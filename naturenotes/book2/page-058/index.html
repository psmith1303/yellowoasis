12.3.61
Pyefleet Channel &amp; Park Farm Wood

With Brian Frost along the sea wall at Pyefleet channel.

The first bird we saw was a Snipe, which we flushed from the flooded fields it flew over the saltings & away.

Up and over the sea wall where Redshank few off wildly disturbing Curlew & Widgeon & Teal. Over the sea wall, out over the marsh to the left were a small flock of Shelduck. They seemed paired up & the males with their very bright red bills were bowing and stretching their necks, the flew & circled around & then settled again. There were many more on the water in the channel all the way up as far as the red warning board. Quite a few more Widgeon & Teal were also in parties, mostl along the opposite bank. A heron flew up from the dyke & moved up further on.

I was disappointed not to see the pair of Kestrels which were always quartering over the marsh, on most visits I’ve made before. Kestrels are quite rare now about the whole of N.E. Essex, as are Little Owls.

Brian & I stopped for coffee at the pillbox mentioned in other notes, we could go no further as mortar practice was taking place in the ranges a few hundred feet away. Whilst sitting down, about five low swimming birds suddenly got up & flew down channel they puzzled me for a few moments & then I realized they were Red breasted Mergansers about five all told & two more later on.
