…then two more Avocets. Gadwell were flyng in & swimming as was a Shoveller & more Shelduck. Redshanks were more common here & a couple or three spotted Redshanks. Swallows dived in & flew out of the sluice. More Dunlin on the muddy lagoon & Coots. Two more Herons, one wading & one standing in the reeds, well camouflaged. Ringed Plover were on the beach & two nests of four eggs were found. Another Wheatear by the sluice. From inside the shore hide we saw another Marsh Harrier which gave us very good views. Spotted Medick was quite abundant & the Sea pea was showing quite well. No Sea Holly yet. We made our way back by about seven & reached the cars. We had all a very full & rather exciting day & very much enjoyed.

[Taped in: Ink drawing 8.7cm x 12.5cm described ‘Avocets. Havergate Island. Suffolk. Signed AKW and dated 30.4.61.]
