20.4.59
Lexden.

Accompanied by R. Leavett & A. Wood we met up with S. Parker by appointment to visit a nesting site of Nuthatches. The wood is situated about fifty feet from the Colchest er Bypass near Glen Avenue.

The actual hole of the nest was in an old Ash tree & as the entrance was quite small already the birds hadn’t done much “plastering” around it. We stepped back under a canopy of Holly & waited for the bird to appear. Whilst doing this we had good views a GoldCrest which looked a little tattered on the crown. A movement of a bird up in the neighbouring tree alerted us to see the Nuthatches. One flew and made its way into the hole and stayed there. We heard the other calling out & after very good views of this bird which was quite bright it flew away.

After leaving this small wood on our way out I picked up some plants I did not know & taking a specimen home identified them as Golden Saxifrage - of which there were two or more clumps on this dampish patch. S. Parker led us through fields of “Cuckoo” flowers & just blooming buttercups to see a nest of a Long tailed tit, but unfortunately the nest was partially destroyed & of the fourteen eggs originally there, seven remained. Probably the work of boys, damn them. S.P. climbed a tree with a large cavity…
