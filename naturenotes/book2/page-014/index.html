Nature Notes 1959. Page 014

There were a number of Mallards sitting in the reed edges, with a few Coot swimming nearby.

The weather turned cloudy by now and it was a very cold wind from the west. We moved down toward the ringing station, to see a ♂ & ♀ Stonechat which has been observed all this ‘58⁄59 winter. Along a Blackthorn hedge adjoining we saw a dozen or more Goldfinches, brilliant in the sunshine. Once or twice we saw single Greenfinches in the hedgerow, & flying.

Many Gulls, Blackbirds, Starlings, and Rooks were seen as well as pairs and individuals of L.T.Tits and Robins and Jackdaws. At Layer village in an orchard were a lot of rotten apples, and feeding on these were the usual Starlings, Blackbirds and Thrushes and on Redwing. Here and there I noticed various Blue & Great tits searching boughs of trees and hedgerow bushes. I arrived home at 2.00pm.

Whilst working at Witham on 14/2/59, during a very fine warm day, I saw the first Small Tortoiseshell butterfly, which looked in good condition.
