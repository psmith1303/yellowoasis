8.9.59 Donyland Wood

With R.L. & George Evans to do a round of sugaring along the main pathway, from the Mersea Road, to as far as the small stream.

The evening was warm & no moon & conditions seemed right. After sugaring a good number of trees, I showed G.E. some nice ferns growing along the dried up stream. Little & tawny owl were calling loudly & one little owl was seen.

The patches which we toured twice yielded two Red Underwings, & two or three Great green grasshoppers & one Long horned Grasshopper (Platycleis Grisea). Copper underwing were plentiful, as were Square spot rustic, one Angleshades & one Swordgrass. A couple of small dark blue beetles & one light brown beetle was also taken. The Large yellow Underwing was common. We left by 10 oclock.
