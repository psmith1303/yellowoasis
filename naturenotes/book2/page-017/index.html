I continued a short way along the naze back water beach and here I saw a party of Turnstone in summer plumage, very handsome, with two grey plover and one Sanderling and here and there were pairs of Ringed Plover.

Quite an experience was had when I first spotted a ♂ Eider preening way out on a mud flat alone. When after a few minutes I came back this bird was nearly on the beach where I was able to approach it to about 25ft and make a sketch before it took to the water. There was no visible sign of injury, but this tame act seemed to good for a normal healthy shy sort of bird as is the drake eider. After this I worked my way back, not seeing any other unusual birds. Walking back along the beach, I noticed all the way along the cliffs - an abundance of Coltsfoot, as we discovered last year. I departed at approximately 12 oclock.

29.3.59 Abberton Res.

A short visit to the resavoir on a cold and windy day. As to be expected, most birds were under cover. The species I did see were as follows. Reed Bunting. Coots. G.C.Grebes. Teal. Heron. Tufted Ducks. Mute swans. Rooks. B.H.Gulls. Mallard. My visit’s purpose was the hope of seeing arriving migrants, but still the wind are not favourable.
