23 April 1961
Park Farm Wood

With G.E. & B.F. to the wood. G.E. & B.F. arrived earlier & I made it by 8.15am. I wandered through the wood on my own for a while, as George & Brian were down by the river. Much bird song greeted me, G.Woodpecker, Starling, Chiffchaff, Robins, Rooks, Jackdaws Wrens & tits & many other all mingling in one chorus. Through to the Western side of the wood I stopped under & lovely large spreading oak tree & watched Nuthatches peeping above me, the a Kestrel-like bird swooped in & settled in the top of the later leafed Ash tree & then began to cuckoo this was my first ‘61 view of the cuckoo. Much bird activity was further enhanced by a nice view of my first ‘61 Blackcap. Pigeons flew over & clapped their wings & pheasants & Partridges called out. A G.S. Woodpecker started drumming in the previous places, we had heard before, & the yaffle sounded out many times all round the wood. The weather started off quite bright & fairly warm, but at about this time a shower of rain started for a while, but soon cleared up, during the rest of the morning it was very sunny.

George & Brian came through the wood & we walked back to where the Nuthatches were pecking out a hole previously mentioned in the notes of before, I was at it again & flew on & off as tits & starlings came near, this gave us very good views & we heard the calls very clearly, finally it flew off, so we went through the woods & down to the river as far as the pipe, we sat on this & had coffee…
