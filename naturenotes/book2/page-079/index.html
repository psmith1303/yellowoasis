…& replayed a tape recording of “The New Auralians field day.”

From here we followed the river, seeing a ♀ reed bunting on the vegetation on the river side, on the clump of Willow trees, tits were in these & Linnets flew over. Accross the marshy fields we saw birds flying onto a fence on getting closer we saw they were our first Yellow Wagtails ♂♂ & ♀♀, of which during the morning we saw several pairs. One probable first year bird had us guessing for a while as to its identify it had no yellow at all, it main plumage was grey with a slight eye stripe & two slight wing bars & underparts uniform grey, later we saw an identical bird but with slight yellow underparts, which gave us the answer.

We carried on accross the field & to the copse of poplars, looking for woodpeckers nests - no luck. George kept near a singing nightingale & made a recording, whilst Brian & I kept searching, during this time we had spells of very warm sunshine. A Heron flew down to the ditch & was being mobbed by a Crow or Rook as it flew off again, which it gradually overtook & flew on. A Green Woodpecker flew through the copse & veered off & away when it saw us. Another we saw, across the river flew up to an oak by the bridge. Shelduck & Mallard were flying around all morning & two more took up when we went back across a ditch.

Large patches of Cuckoo flowers were everywhere & here & there a few buttercups.
