Nature Notes 1959. Page 088

…♂ perched higher up. After our snack & coffee we decided to move away from the pasture area & down to where the clump of Willow trees grow by the river & old tark trap. As we approached, I saw a Carrion Crow, leave a nest we had discovered earlier, so to get over to the “island” we had to walk down stream & over the wooden bridge & back along the river. I climbed up to the nest, it was quite difficult & the strong wind made the trees sway a lot, but I reached the nest okay & peered over the edge to see a nicely formed cup well lined, containing one newly hatched chick & one egg, which was cracked & the chick inside moving the shell up & down. In these conditions a photographic record was to difficult.

From this area we back tracked to the bridge & noted Buchanans Speedwell & Pennywort & Red Campion & primroses & numerous small flowers among these spotted Medick, carrying on over the “shrike field area” I pointed out one or two plants of Early purple orchis, again as he didn’t see them earlier.

A noise attracted us to the river, where we saw the Garrison Beagle being exercised, these were running wild & we decided to go back through the wood up to the long tailed tits nest which we found had been pulled to pieces by a predator, as was probably the case of a Blackbirds nest nearby. The Cuckoo flowers here nearly all had Orange tip eggs on them, these we collected for breeding or rather rearing. Through the thickets & down to the river again on the way stopping for a Whitethroat recording. More Cuckoo flower in the lower mead, but no orange tip eggs here,…
