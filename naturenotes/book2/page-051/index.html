Sunday 12.2.61
Leewick

With Russell Leavett to Leewick. The weather was rainy & the clouds cleared now & then to give sunny periods. There was a slight breeze.

We arrived at about 10.10, & after parking the motor-bike we walked along the beach towards Brightlingsea disturbing Redshanks as we went. The Cormorants as usual were sitting on theposts in the sea & they flew off in the direction we were going. We went into a hut at the old sand works & left our food bags & carried on. More Cormorants passed us towards the mud flats which the outgoing tide exposed.

Feeding along the waters edge were quite a few Sanderling & parties of about eight Oyster Catchers, which were piping away merrily. Two Turnstones were feeding & we watched them awhile as they turned many quite large stones. Half way along this beach we saw Bar tailed Godwit feeding with two Grey Plovers & more Sanderling. Gulls were flying about most of the time including (Herring, G.B.B. & Black headed) as well as more Cormorants passed by, further along were more parties of Oyster Catchers.
