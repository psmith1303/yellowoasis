Nature Notes 1959. Page 044

…beautiful spot where of course we were after the Lulworth skipper which was not very plentiful. The Chalk hill blue was quite common here & the Brown Argus. I only saw one Marbled White & one or two common blues. Here one again the Grayling was plentiful. There were quite a few Dark Green Frittilaries worm & egg laying, but going very fast in the breeze on the very very steep slopes.

We saw a party of 4 to five immature Stonechats, & plenty of Linnets & Goldfinches. We saw one hawk which could possibly have been a Peregrine, but really to distant for sure identification. Herring Gulls one again on the cliffs. Many more Lingards here as well as grasshoppers & Red-ants. The plant life was good, with many heads of the Carline Thistle about. Clustered bellflower abundant. On the plateau were a few plants here & there of Dyers Greenweed a nice yellow Brown looking plant, there was once again the normal commoner chalk plants. I should mention one other plant quite common in these areas, the Common Valerian. I also saw some Montbresia growing in a wild state but I doubt its being naturally wild, as can be said for some Evening primrose at Lulworth. During train journeys I noticed quite a lot of Cottongrass along the stretch of the New Forest. The weather during this whole period was very changeable being cloudy sunny & warm. Windy & showery, but we enjoyed it most of the time.
