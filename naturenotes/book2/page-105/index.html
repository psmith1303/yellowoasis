Abberton Resavoir & Park farm Area 6.8.61

With Brian Frost, whom I rendezvoused with at 6.30am by the resavoir gates on the Layer to Tollshunt D’Arcy road. Leaving my M/cycle, we went on his combination around the perimeter of the resavoir, holding a permit to do so.

At the very first we saw many Coot & Mallard & Swans & circling overhead were many hirundines, as I had noted a few hundreds of Sand Martins on telephone wire by Layer-church. The weather was quite bright with cloud patches & morning sun, but very cold. We went all the way round seeing as we went many Yellow Wagtails, quite a few terns & gulls & Great-Crested Grebes. Migrant Common & Green Sandpiper were fairly numerous, we never saw any Wheatears. We saw one ♀ Red breasted Marganser in eclipse as it couldn’t only paddle away from us very quickly. Lapwings we every few hundred or fifty feet along the sides. In one ploughed field there were many Yellow Wagtails & young birds with half dozen pairs of Turtle-doves.
