Sunday 16 April 1961
Park farm Wood & Leewick Essex

With G.E. & B.F. & a cousin of G.E. an American named Dicky in his car to Leewick after he had collected us from park farm wood, where we had been since about 7.30. On arrival in the wood we proceeded to our normal spot & watched starlings in the holes in a birch tree. Not much activity by the nuthatch tree & Green Woodpeckers were calling around the wood, we still searched hard for a nest but no go. The G.S.W. was drumming again & we saw a ♀ fly accross to an old oak. Three Green Woodpeckers flew near park farm & we watched through binoculars, as they flew towards & past us, giving us lovely views. We went round the road to our bikes & at that time Dicky came.

We proceeded from the wood to Leewick in Dickie’s car. Upon arrival we had coffee & eats & then started walking along the beach. In the saltings we saw young shoots of Golden Samphire & young Yellow Horned poppy. There was plenty of Bladder campion & a few plants of Sandwort, more of Ragwort & Thrift. A Cormorant flew across the sea from Brightlingsea area . Shelduck & Mallard were flying about quite a bit, so were Curlews & Redshanks & B.H. Gulls.
