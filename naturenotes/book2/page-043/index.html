…found the Lulworth skiller at this sport. We found no abberations.

Our next day was spent in the New Forest at Brokenhurst hoping to get some frittilary butterflies (Valerjina ab:.) we only saw one which flew by at a terrific pace. The Speckled Wood was common here & J.F. took two or three, other commoner butterflies were plentiful, Meadow brown & Small M.B. Small Skipper & Gatekeeper. Not a lot of plants, but Canadian flea-bane was quite plentiful & one very nice plant of Tutsan with berries. We believe we saw a Sparrow Hawk dash across the rides once or twice & a lot of noise from smaller birds probably mobbing. Bullfinches were heard. One or two large nests of Wood ants were found.

We decided the following day to have a look over at Studland & on a small part of the Heath. On the heath are sand dunes of lovely silver sand & huge areas of Heather. We were pleased to find nearly as much Bog myrtle. Great Dock was plentiful in a small stream. We came across a few very nice Sand Lingards. Here the Grayling was very common dancing in two’s and threes over the warm sand dunes. After this a boat trip around Poole Harbour revealed quite a few Oyster Catchers & Curlews & Redshank. With Herring Gulls, B.H. Gulls & Cormorants flying & swimming around. One L.B.B. Gull was perched on a post quite close as we sailed by.
