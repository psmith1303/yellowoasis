…round & in & out of the trees, a little earlier on.

Jays & pheasants were making calls in the thickets & I gradually drew nearer the drumming three which I suspected & paused under a Blackthorn, two Long tailed tits appeared & then two more, at which the first pair got agitated & scorned, nearly were several large clumps of bright gorse & I looked in one or two & fo9und a Wrens nest which was empty, however on the other side of the bush was the L.T.T. nest which contained eggs, which I felt with my finger, I stepped away a few yards & watched the pair of birds return okay.

The elusive woodpecker had stopped its drumming. I went back to G.E. & B.F. whom had made some nice recordings of Nuthatch calls & also of the birds chipping round the hole. B.F. did this by holding the mike on the tree trunks & operating the set by G.E.’s directions, the tape turned out quite well, with a laugh of the Green Woodpecker on it which called out as I arrived.

Telling BF & GE the discovery, they were keen to come & see the nest as it was well situated for a hide & photography. Both inspected the nest & we were all pleased as we watched the birds reurn again with food.

In a small area around this nest we found another blackbirds nest with three eggs & also the same number in a Thrushes nests. Another thrust nest also nearby held five eggs, we were doing well & pleased. We searched about in the undergrowth & came across pipe which were installed to encourage foxes to breed.
