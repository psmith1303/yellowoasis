9.4.61
Park farm Wood

With G.E. & B.F. we arrived in the wood at about 7am. G.E. & myself arrived first. Right away we entered the wood & left our bait by the trees. From here we went through the wood & out of it lower down & then into the thickly covered field of brambles, blackthorn & scrub. This sloping field overlooks the Roman River valley & from here we watched three pairs of Shelducks, the males doing their little courtship bobbing of the head to the females, just then two ♂ Wild Ducks (Mallard) winged in, & settled near the Shelducks. We carried on down & along the river, where a ♂ & ♀ Reed Bunting were seen on the opposite bank, further along we spotted a Heron coming down in a far stream. Up as far as the pipes accross the river & a pair of Blue tits seemed very anxious, just then we spotted another Heron in flight at which the first bird took off & flew away in another direction. The bill of the first bird was quite orange & the other was much lighter, perhaps ♂ & ♀.

We crossed the pipe & was making for the hedge where we had found a L.S. Woodpecker’s nest, when up flew a pair of Mallard, which circled around two or three times. We inspected the Long tailed tits nest & found it empty, the eggs had gone. predators we think, & not children, as the next was still intact if only slightly lopsided.

At this time B.F. arrived & we reentered the wood. We looked at a nest of a Thrush with three eggs. A Blackbirds nest was still unfinished & we continued through the wood.
