Nature Notes 1959. Page 080

…down the river & we saw our first ‘61 House & Sand Martins, the house martin took us a little while to make sure of it as it flew in the sun & could only be seen as a sillouette. We carried on along-side the wood & noted the fallen poplar had a hole in it, which had nesting material in it, a tit perhaps.

Lovely clumps of Archangel were well out in the wood & windflowers were still in bloom, the bluebells looked lovely.

Whilst watching the swallows from the pipes a Kestrel flew from over the wood & across the fields, to settle in a lovely sloped Elm tree for a few minutes & then in again. We hope it will nest in the area, as we believe it is the same bird we have seen two or three times before, in the area over the wood.

We reached our motor cycles & made off, stopping for a little sallow for our Emperor Larva & then home at 1230.

During the evening at home in Stockwell street, I was very pleased to see two swifts my first for ‘61, the date being the 25th. On the Monday evening I saw two more in the same area, but not during the day.
