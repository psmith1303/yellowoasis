7.6.59
Hillwood - Brent Wood. Essex.

With J.F. to visit Hillswood @ Brentwood meeting up with Paul Betts. We caught the 6-3 from North Station & upon arrival at P.B.’s we set off by cycle (taking mine on train) to the woods, primarily to get some specimens of Rosy Marbled Moths. When we reached the spot though the woods where Lily of the Valley grows profusely, we came to a large clear portion covered only by bracken & a few small Silver birch trees. J.F. took photos of the habitat with P.B. & I examining the insects. We watched a ♂ Tree Pipit singing lustily & saw the parachute display flight. By now dusk was approaching & the Rosy Marbled moths began to fly about very quickly making them elusive. We gathered enough of these and some Four Dotted Footman & then our interest was turned to a Woodcock which gave us a perfect silhouette view as it flew by & about us “Roding”. The most exciting event happened just before we were leaving P.B. was further away from J.F. & I, he shouted out something just as we both saw a Nightjar which I shouted out immediately, we also heard it calling but not a full “churring”. It was my first sight of this interesting uncommon Essex bird. We made our way back, & reached home by 11.40pm.
