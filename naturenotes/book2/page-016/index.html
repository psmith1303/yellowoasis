13.3.59

Three rooks nests are well built in St Martin’s Churchyard Colchester.

The pupas of the Grey Carpet moth have kept a constant colour of buff or stone with tinges of light green, so far no changes have occurred.

28-3-59

There are six rooks nests now completed in the St Martins churchyard, Jackdaws appear to be building in the apex of the church roof again this year, the pigeons occupy the tower end of the church.

28.3.59 Walton-on-Naze

After taking my brother to the pier and having a cup of tea, I proceeded on my own to the naze, hoping to see return migrants. Although the wind was wrong-coming from the West, the day was really lovely - quite warm and sunny.

After walking along the cliff top, I turned toward the gorse covered sloping fields and then I was surprised to see a smallish bird, uniform grey and showing a slight reddish tail in flight, this was my first sighting of a black Redstart. I did not see a great deal until nearly over the sea wall.
