Nature Notes 1959. Page 077

…two birds at first, then six more later over the water along the shore. It was nice to see them again as it always is.

Another Cormorant flying over the sea as we left the beach & headed back to the car.

The drive along the small road was made interesting by a Kestrel hovering over the pastures, we stopped & watched it & it flew a little further on & hovered again a final & deadly stoop to catch a vole or some prey, where-upon it flew on a little way to a large tuft, where it stopped to eat is capture for only a few minutes then it was away again. One other interesting bird seen a little farther on was a Wheatear, which was probably moving on.

We had, had quite a nice morning & were well pleased especially seeing the Kestrel as it numbers are quickly decreasing in N.E. Essex anyway.

Reg Arthur reports quite a lot of Mallard over the saltings this year. For only a few gulls were seen on the gullery opposite point clear. We arrived home about 1.30 rather late for B.F. & his dinner.
