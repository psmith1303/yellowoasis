Sunday 19 Feb 1961
Col. Mangle's Wood Berechurch (Park Farm)

With Brian Frost, George Evans & two sons we visited the wood & area above.

The weather was quite foggy at about 9-00 & we entered the private wood to go to a mammal trap which I had set the previous night at 10 o’clock & baited with cheese bread & split peas. To our amazement the trap was sprung but no mammals although the bait had been nibbled & quite a few leaves were in it. We could only assume that the trap was spring by mice & that they were small enough to get through the ½ wire mesh.

We carried on through the wood to the river where we crossed over a broken bridge, on to the hedgerow which contains a lesser S. Woodpecker hole & which B.F. photographed for me, whilst he was doing this G.E. & myself went over along the river. I was standing by an old rotten tree & whith a rush & whir of wings a lovely Kingfisher flew by, upstream. We all carried along the river & B.F. saw a Green Sandpiper & I only heard it the description Brian gave fitted this bird.

We heard and saw the G.S.W. drumming & the L.S.W. as well. The Green. W. seemed everywhere in voice & sight several times. Making our way back to the bikes we took & had our coffee in the wood, from here we inspected probable sights for Squirrel drays, I wandered down the wood again & watched…
