Nature Notes 1959. Page 084

…several Bar tailed Godwits & one Black tailed Godwit, the bar tails had one or two in summer plumage, & very nice as was the Knot looking very rust red in colour. We left this hide & carried on back past the other hides & wardens hut down the island to the furthermost lagoon here from the hide we saw one or two more Avocets wading & feeding & on the nests. A nice summer plumage Grey Plover. A Yellow Wagtail flew over the mud past immature G.B.B. Gulls & Herring Gulls, Swallows dipped & darted about. A pair of Swans floated about lazily & meadow pipits flitted about, then a ♂ Reed Bunting, Skylarks were over above us. One Common Gull was seen. We had our fill here & had enjoyed it enormously, on our way back we saw more little Terns & more Sandwich Terns as they swam on the water, looking quite strange, there were more Oyster Catchers along the banks & a nice Heron wading in shallow water. Again the boat trip was quite nice & we reached the Quay by 4.10.

As the time was still early, we decided to go on to Minsmere, which we did & on arrival had a snack. We walked along the road edge listening to Sedgewarblers & down along part of the beach. Two Marsh Harriers we soaring over the marsh & looked splendid on long wings. On the beach was a dead porpoise nearly six feet long, which had bene there the week before & rather “high”. Back to the reserve wall we walked along the planted Marram Grass & disturbed a nice Wheatear. Our first look over into the lagoons revealed none other…
