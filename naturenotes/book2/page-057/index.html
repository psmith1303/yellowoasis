Brian was photographing some nice looking moss, when the Colonel came out & we chatted with him. He told us that he came out to look for some deer prints, as the previous evening he believed he heard a deer barking. We hope he was right. We walked with him down the track & to the bridge. A Comma butterfly flew over & we saw a moth (unknown species). There were nice patches of celandines lovely in the sun. we left the Col & walked along the river to some Alders & we were delighted to see four Siskins feeding on the cones, only one looked like a ♂. George saw a Kingfisher when the colonel first came out & he flushed a G.S. Woodpecker which flew over into the wood. During the time in the wood we heard Greater & lesser Spotted woodpeckers drumming. The lesser spotted’s hole in the hedge tree was still untouched.

We made our way back across the remains of the old bridge & along side the Col’s wood, at the tope we saw a nice Tortoiseshell butterfly. During the course of the day we heard a few Jays & saw one Magpie.

By this time, at about 1230, it was getting very warm & only a slight breeze, as we left on our motor cycles.
