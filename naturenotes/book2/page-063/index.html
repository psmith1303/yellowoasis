…was really pleased. This was a good Heronry & guarded by the owner & keeper jealously - no publicity desired at all. We hope to make a further visit when the young are hatched.

Most nest’s had their pairs of tree Sparrows which were nesting in the Heron’s nest structure. Also in the wood were Bullfinches, L.T.Tits, Pigeons, Pheasants & Great tits. B. Headed Gulls flew over. We had to leave soon after noon & it was just as well because the weather worsened & we had a heavy downfall of hail & sleet, but we managed to be home by 12.30. I had really enjoyed my first Heronry visit.

Park Farm Wood 26.3.61

With Brian Frost, whom arrived at the wood earlier than I did, which was about 8.15. I parked my motorcycle & walked through the wood from the gate end. I made for the spot near where a tree with nesting material from a hole can be seen. I took off my haversack & Binoculars & got ready the tape recorder & started recording bird song around me, whilst the wind wasn’t to high & did not interfere with the recording. I wanted to get the Chiffchaff’s song, a bird was singing above me at the time, however when the tape was replayed Starlings & a Robin came out very clear, with distant noises of Crows, Rooks & sheep. After a few minutes I heard a curlew call which heralded the appearance of B.F.
