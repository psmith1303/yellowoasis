Nature Notes 1959. Page 052

…Great Crested Grebes, as well as two assumed C. Scoter, further out than these was one Red Throated Diver.

There were quite a few dead birds along the tide line & these were. 2 Shelducks, 2 C. Scoters ♂ & ♀. 1 Curlew. 1 GBB (immature). 1 Brent Goose. 1 Red Throated Diver (oiled badly). 1 Common Gull & the remains of a Redshank or Dunlin.

A party of eight Snow Buntings were seen at the end of the sand on shingle bank & they appeared to be ♀♀s & immature birds, feeding in the gravel. Coming back however, we had excellent views from 7-8 ft of a lovely ♂. The bill was very noticeably yellow & small cone shaped. This bird was alone.

We saw a ♂ Greenfinch & feeding nearby was a Goldfinch, other finches we noted were Linnets & Sparrows.

After having lunch we waded in the low water pools for various types of seaweeds, which Russ collected and hoped to identify. We collected about a dozen different species.

We tried the other beach towards Jaywick, & saw some more Brents, with gulls & more Cormorants in flight. Coming back towards the sand-works we met J. Richardson & N. Syer who were just out strolling.

Out departure was at about 4-40 & as we got over the sea wall “hump” in the road we saw a Short Eared Owl flapping over the marsh.

It was quite a nice day out & we arrived home at 5.35.
