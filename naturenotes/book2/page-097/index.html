12.6.61
Park farm Wood &amp; Area

With G.E. B.F. & D. we arrived by 7.30am & parked m/cycles & straight away into the shrike field, here we saw Bullfinches & Whitethroats & Linnets & a Spotted flycatcher, further down by the river we saw a Sedge warbler & two Tree Sparrows, whilst swallows hawked up & down. With the arrival of Mr Dickie we crossed over the pipe & along to the hedgerow where the G.W.’s nest was, we carried on past here & along to the big Elm tree where the Kestrel is nesting, we all climbed up & were delighted to see the 5 young looking quite big & healthy. B.F. took another photograph, as we want to record the stages of growth, they were a fortnight old this day, their down was grey now & not white, the feet & cere showed up quite yellow. One young was photographed in the hand, to give some idea of size at two weeks. Well pleased we left here & walked back to the woodpecker hedgerow, when we neared the suspected nest a G.Woodpecker flew out from the hedge & I eagerly climbed to the hole only to find it was not a proper excavation, so we searched along the hedge & I looked in an old hole found previously & heard the young woodpeckers inside these we recorded & looked at through G.E. patent light nicknamed a Shuftie-scope, which worked very well. The young were still naked & blind & numbered five.
