Nature Notes 1959. Page 049

…took a couple of dozen larvae of Agrotis Repi (Sand Dart) nearly every plant revealed this flecky brownish sand coloured caterpillar, of which from specimens taken at Leewick J.F. received a slight nip from the mandibles of this species.

Parties of waders along the shore were summer plumage Dunlin, about six in number with Ringed Plover. Further on were the same number approx. of Oyster catchers & one L.B.B. Gull.

Although not a great variety of birds were seen we all enjoyed the morning & the nice area of marsh & shore line, being pleased with the Larvae in particular. Our arrival home was about 11.45.

Backdating an entry from May 1959, emerging an uncommon moth taken at my M/V lamp at 39 E. Stockwell St Colchester & verified by J.F. on 12.9.59 the specimen was in good condition & set by myself. Probably a first record for Colchester of the Maple Prominent.
