Nature Notes 1959. Page 106

…Great Crested Grebe, then a G.H.Gull & then an unidentified duck with head missing & web footed. Merganser?

We left this area by nineish & quite cold decided to go to park farm area, where upon arrival we had coffee & a snack. We walked down the sloping field where a spring is running & flushed a Snipe which pleased Brian & it zig-zaged & weaved away from us. A Lapwing & two Moorhens also scurried away as we continued along the river past the pipe & bridge a quick look at the bridge & leaf cutter bee holes. A nice look at a small copper & common blue pleased us, as we walked up into the wood. Here the undergrowth & foliage had grown thicker & Common enchanters nightshade was indeed quite thick & common.

Blackcaps & Chiffchaffs still lingered their songs & we were pleased to hear them & the harsh jay notes & that of a Green Woodpecker. We went to the east side of the wood, where were delighted to see a Nuthatch & a tree-creeper & to find a Jay (probably a young bird with feather quills on head & chin) at the foot of a tree with its head & beak held fast by the grass & roots at the foot of a Birch tree, it hadn’t been dead much more than the previous day as it was intact with its eyes still in, we pulled quite hard to remove it & kept the bird for its skull & wings & tarsus. We had to be going as time was getting on, I was just leaving Brian as a Tawny Owl flashed over the road into the Wood. The time was about 10-40.

Of note was the pair of Kestrels we saw hovering near the cottages, upon our arrival, probably “our” breeding pair.
