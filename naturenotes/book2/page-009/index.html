18th January 1959
Abberton to Mersea

Accompanied by R.L. and A.W. we went to Abberton Resavoir via Bounstead road. We watched a party approx 30 of Fieldfares, the first time for A.W. We had good views as the birds flew from the hedge-row to the ploughed field where they were well camouflaged, grey rump very distinctive. Many Blackbirds particularly were seen today, with all the usual commoner species, with nice views of the Greenfinch. A.W. obtained a head for a skull, of a Magpie near Bounstead Brook.

On arrival at the Layer-de-le-Haye causeway over the resavoir we found a dead G. C. Grebe, of which, I had the head for a skull, AW had the feet. Still frozen over, the water did not have many birds on it, except large parties of Coot, a few G.B.B. Gulls & Herring Gull. A large party of Ducks well out contained Widgeon (which we heard whistling out) Teal, Mallard, & Tufted Duck. The mist hung over this flock and we did not have good views. Two other species seen here were Snipe, & L.T.Tit.

We cycled round to the other causeway, where much the same was the scene, only better views of Mallard, which looked comical when they came in to land and sliding on the ice.
