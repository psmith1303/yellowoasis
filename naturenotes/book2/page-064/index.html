Nature Notes 1959. Page 064

…I went off to try to better a Chiffchaff song, which was quite good when replayed, also the calls of mating Jays.

I returned to Brian and we both got in the 4’ x 4’ hide. We had heard & recorded Green Woodpeckers calling & the G.S. Woodpecker drumming a few minutes previously, when we heard the former call out again & saw it fly into a holly tree, I mimiced its call & it jerked its head up & looked about & called out, once again, to which I replied, its response to this was to fly on a tree very close to the hide & giving us good views. It was trying to find one of its own. I believe our movement caused it to fly off & it made a small circuit & landed back on & oak tree, more calling made this bird frantic, finally it flew to another part of the wood. I continued to mimic its laughing call. We were very surprised after a few minutes of calling, to have replies from at least three birds & even further to our surprise we were excited when three birds landed near the bole of a medium-sized chestnut tree. We watched them chase each other & they climbed & descended very quickly in a spiral fashion round the tree, this carried on for a full three minutes before they flew off altogether accross the wood, after which we continued to hear them. This was to my mind a courtship display of two ♂♂ & one ♀. There wasn’t any sign of hostility towards each other. It did prove that the woodpeckers can be called up okay. During the rest of the morning we saw & heard them several times more.
