Nature Notes 1959. Page 069

Previous page Next page

…the pine-wind breaks. A few Chaffinches & Linnets here & more pheasants & partridges, a Green Woodpecker flew up & away from the bracken, we heard this bird several times during the rest of the day.

We spotted quite a flock sparrows with Tree sparros among them. We decided after this to have our lunch early & it rained nastily whilst we sat under a hedge & had it. After this we moved over the ground again to the sandy area which is eroded & here to our delight were a nice pair of Wheatears - our first for ‘61. We watched these display flight. I found a beetle on the heather, which looked to me to be a Dor beetle, but it seems early for these, to be identified by Nigel Cumming.

Once again we saw a Stone Curlew as it flew off out of sight on long barred wings.

As the weather worsened, the visibility became poorer, so we decided we should be going on our way home, we had fair views of the birds we had come to see & were thankful the weather hung out fine enough for us to see them. We got quite wet around our feet going home but we enjoyed our visit, which was G.E.’s first.

A small white flower was blooming & so was a Cranesbill of a type to be identified.

The beetle was identified as the Minotaur Beetle (Typhaeus Thyphaeus) by N/C.
