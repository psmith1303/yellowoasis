10.5.59 Orfford sea walls to Havergate
On a beautiful warm morning, my wife & children & I went by motor cycle to Orfford chiefly on my part to see the Avocets.
On arrival at lunch time we found a nice spot & had lunch, after which we proceeded to walk along the sea-wall observing Yellow Wag tails along the water filled dyke, further along was a ♂ Reed Bunting & Shelducks in an ajoining field. At this point I linke dup with a young naturalist from Co. Durham, down on a holiday & there to see the Avocet’s. He accompanied me to a convenient position on the sea wall where we sat to watch birds. After ten minutes & no Avocets he rejoined his friends, just as the first Avocet flew up & mobbed a Gull. After this many more were seen flying up, one came particularly near & we had very good views. A Short Eared owl flew up & started quartering the ground giving good views as it hovered & swooped.
