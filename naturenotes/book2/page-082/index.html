…from an old woodpecker hole on the way to a piece of open heath covered in gorse & broom & bramble, the two former were very brilliant & more profuse than I had ever seen them before. Two nests of the Linnets were found in gorse bushes, each contained four eggs. over this piece of land nearly stepping on Adders & seeing one lingard we went across to a conifer wood where we found a curious outcrop or sand working which was exactly like Red Crag. There were a few Sand Martins here to & plants of Cransebill. A Wheatear was seen as it flew off & another Red Squirell giving us very good views as it ran up & crossed the pines. I found one unknown chrysalis on the ground surface by a plant of Ragwort. Green Woodpeckers were still calling loudly & we flushed a Yellowhammer & Whitethroat. R. Leavett collected a few Diptera specimens.

We left the area & continued to Orford, where, at the Quay we got a boat, with Reg Partridge, warden of Havergate Island, at the helm. It was a pleasant trip up the River Ord. Many B.H. Gulls were about & we passed a small party of Bar tailed Godwits on the mud, & a little further on, one or two Oyster Catchers. Lovely views were had of a Short Eared Owl as it quartered the saltings, another was spotted as it squated on the sea wall. Yet another was see, as soon as we disembarked & went to the wardens hut, this one was sitting on a post with a pheasant on the ground near it. After a few moments another mobbed it & both flew off.
