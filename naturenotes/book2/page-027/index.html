9.5.59
Abberton Resavoir

With my wife and R.L. visited the resavoir. On route here noticed & watched our first ‘59 Turtle Doves. Many Swifts & Swallows were noted, as well as numerous Whitethroats which seemed everywhere.

At the resavoir we noted Tufted Duck, Mallard, Pochard & G.C. Grebes, Coot & Yellow Wagtail & my first ‘59 Common Terns. J. Cowlin came along & told R.L. & myself of some Whimberel & Black tern on the main parts. Some c.a. 50 Whimberel & a dozen B. Terns. We made our way quickly round & proceeded by foot walking round the roadway, where we saw two Shelducks flying toward us, with the most scarlet coloured bills I’ve ever seen. We were lucky enough to spot about four Black Terms - the first I’ve seen, at some distance flying & skimming over the water. We did not see the Whimberels. Going home via Layer de la Haye we stopped at the hill by the church which overlooks the water & watched House Martins. We heard a curious trilling whistle, which the next day at Orfford Ness I found out was the call of the Whimberel.

Leaving the resavoir, we stopped for a snack at Friday wood farm & R.L. & I walked around looking for the R.B. Shrikes - unlucky, but we were fortunate to see a lovely ♂ Whinechat in same area as last year, again many Whitethroats were seen, on or two Yellow Hammer & one each of Yellow Wagtail & Pied & later another pair of Yellow Wagtails on the swampy land.
