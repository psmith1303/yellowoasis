The plants in the wood had not advanced much in a week except Lords & Ladies. The bluebells promise a very good show. A few plants of primroses remain in the middle of the wood. We departed about 12-20 & when leaving we saw one Bullfinch in Ramparts Lane.

5.3.61

First Part Resavoir latter Friday Wood At 6.30am over to Abberton Resavoir with my wife P.W. The weather was very cold & frosty & the water was cover over with fog reducing visibility to about 30ft. A bird flew up as we stopped & I believe it was a ♂ Smew, a Heron flew up as well. There were quite a few Tufted duck ♂ & ♀ G.C. Grebes which were giving their courtship display & calls. Two Ringed Plover were feeding at the water edge. Many minnows were seen under the bridge, they were lying on their sides & gradually right themselves, it seemed as though they were playing a game. Hundreds of coot were packed along the water’s edge & moved out as we went near. The call of the widgeon was very plain & heard a lot. On the other causeway, one Snipe flew up & away. Two Shelducks flew over. A Cock Reed Bunting was feeding at the waters edge & so was a Pied Wagtail.

The main reason for the visit, was to see the newly formed heronry in the trees, behind Gen Wainwrights…
