Nature Notes 1959. Page 093

On arrival in France, after flying from Southend the first bird we saw was a skylark. The airport seemed very quiet & the dull weather didn’t help.

With G.E. & son we drove accross France in a Standard 8 car, and all our gear stored in the boot. We had hired two Sandfly tents in Colchester & these we intended to sleep in, but supplies were only light as we expected to get Bed & breakfast in Germany. Stop at Le Bateau for tea & Nightingale.

The first few miles of France were not very interesting & we motored all night, with a stop of two hours. Magpies we about the most seen bird en route. It was interesting to go through the French villages & study the architecture & the streets with no footpaths. Very noticeable was elderly women working on the painting & even ditching round the houses & more types of manual labour. The wine places or cafes stayed open quite late, & opened early in the morning. In one village men were drinking in them at approx 6.45.

Our next stop, the next day 20.5.61 was at an old gravel pit working near Metz. We had breakfast & took a “stretcher” & looked around picking specimens of (“Crested Cowheat?) that were lovely as was the Milkwort & many other plants including Wayfaring tree, Red Campion various sedges. A moth was captured coming out of the C. Cowheat and a beetle nearby. As we prepared to leave here I spotted our first Buzzard of the day. Continuing on to [Germany] a nice view was had of the ♂ R.B.Shrike sitting on telephone wires. More Buzzards & three Kites were seen as we journeyed on having very good views of both species, flying & perching.

Nearing Strasbourg on the G/F border, we were surprised to see a White Stork feeding in a cultivated field.

We passed over the border uneventfully & into Germany.
