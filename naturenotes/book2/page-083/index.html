…party of twelve who were visiting the sanctuary. The warden kindly split us up in two parties & we were on our own in each hide. From the first hide, very well made & maintained we had lovely views of the Avocets from very close range, as the fed, waded & flew & sitting on their nests or standing in the water knee deep preening. I thought the bird looked its best when in flight or preening. The number there were estimated at 50 prs & a lot of predation was done by the 3,500 gulls in the colony as told by the warden. We had half an hour or more in this hide & then carried on to the next, where again we saw more Avocets & B.H.Gulls & a good view of the Sandwich Tern colony, there was quite a bit of nose from these & gulls. The Terns were more or less nesting side by side with the gulls & here & there an odd Avocet nested. Many pairs or single birds of the Shelducks were dotted evenly showing off their beautiful plumage & brilliant bills the ♂♂ bobbing their heads in display. Fighting & copulation was common place among the Gulls. A few duck were here & there among these were Mallard, Pintail, Godwell and Shoveller of which we had good views. Redshanks flew here & there & Moorhens, Curlew & Whimberel. On to another hide & here I stopped on the sea wall, (the others carried on) to record the Sandwich terms as they flew over with sand eels, I managed to get a good recording of the Avocets as these flew over me.
