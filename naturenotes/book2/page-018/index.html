30.3.59
East Mersea

The weather was calm & overcast but not cold, so with my son David & Rose & Thomas Scott we visited East Mersea. On arrival I noticed two pairs of lapwings in courtship flight, tumbling & wheeling and crying in a field to the fore of the sea wall - a prospective nesting site.

I was hoping to see a mass gathering of Divers off Colne point, as these were reported to me this previous Saturday as being 50 or 60 Red throated & one Great Northern, the former some of which were in partial summer plumage.

Swimming & drifting on the calm sea were a few mixed Gulls & two or three parties of Brent Geese. I saw four in flight, going towards the river Colne. In a nearby plouged field were a couple of Shelducks & nearby a party of gulls. I saw a little display from two Ringed Plover on the shingle beach, but no other waders around.

Growing along the sea wall was a patch of wild onion or garlic. I noticed Lesser Celandine were well in flower, some nearly over. One or two bushes of Lupins seemed to be growing wild, as they were as well in a small field near houses.
