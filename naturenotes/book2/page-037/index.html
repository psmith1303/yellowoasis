After leaving this area we made for Tiptree heath to look at the botany of this heath. On sitting down for a snack & drink we were upon the plant Lousewort, from then on we found many interesting plants as follows, Milkwort, C.Sedge, Cuckoo flower & (larva of Orange tip on it) Tormentil, Field Buttercup. White Bedstraw, Dwarf Sallow or Creeping Willow, Star of Bethlehem (probable escape.) Stitchwort, Cross leaved heath & Sing. Whilst looking at the Sallow or Willow a lovely freshly emerged Elephant Hawk moth was secured by Alan Wood found low down near the food plant Rose Bay Willow herb. Many larva of the Small Tortoiseshell were found on stinging nettle. J.F. found one egg of the Puss-moth on Aspen. Here again the Emperor moth was flying around very fast. J.F. R.L. and & I were the last to leave & the party were waiting for us on the coach, we arrived back to Colchester by 6.30pm.

The weather was warm & sunny all day, slight S.E. breeze.
