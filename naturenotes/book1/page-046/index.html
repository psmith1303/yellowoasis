29.6.57
Walton Backwaters

With Neal & John of C.N.H.S. and a Mr Snoxle an official bird ringer. I went to the “gull” island around Walton backwaters, here approximately two thousand Black headed Gulls (Larus Ridibundus) nest we set out to ring as many young as possible. The weather was very warm, & still, we encountered the first rivulet, the tide running out we crossed safely, Mr Snoxle at the rear. The second creekway was reached & John, & I crossed safely but Mr Snoxle got well, and truly sunk in the mud up to his thighs, on hurrying back across to assist him, & Neal, I half fell in the filthy smelling mud which went in my mouth nose ears, & eyes, & all up the front of my shirt, but we continued on, & reached the first scattered nests of the first colony. 1 nest. 1 egg.

We had great success in catching & ringing forty eight young B. H. Gulls. I did three which were the first birds I’ve ever rung, a great experience for me. …
