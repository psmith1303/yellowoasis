…to the beach where we saw the first of the Turnstone, & the Sanderlings, my first view of this species. Very fast, & light on the mantle white underparts, approx six in number.

The weather continued badly, but we carried on, & saw more Turnstones & Sanderlings. Took shelter for a while & had crisps. Saw among the Gulls, & L.B.B. in flight? & Common & B.H. Gulls. One Cormorant flew away from us, & a Heron in flight. The weather worsened, & we sheltered in sight of many waders, huge flocks I couldn’t see clearly. Curlews in flight, & Redshanks, & more Turnstones. We found in the sea-wall a Bristly Ox-tongue my first noticed sight quite a few plants & later found the Glass-wort. Horsetail, & Smooth Ox-tongues large flock of Pigeons, & Lapwings. At this time decided to walk back from East Mersea to the Motor-cycle. We were very wet, but happy. Accross Strood stopped to look at the Curlew’s feeding in the mud. Arrived home 1 o’clock.
