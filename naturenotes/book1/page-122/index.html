31.8.58
Walton Naze

With R.L. arrived at the naze at Walton at 5.50 to watch for migrating birds. There was nothing terribly exciting the first migrant was a Sedge Warbler on the briar, & Gorse covered fields. We left these fields and continued in increasing rain to the E.B.W.S. hide along the sea front, & saltings. Here after a snack, & tea we observed 35 Cormorants which when the mist lifted we found were “drying off” on a exposed sand bank. There were numerous Gulls, Black head Herring, & G.B.B. Good numbers of Ringed plover, & Oyster Catchers, & Redshanks. A party of Turnstones flew settled & flew of again. After some time the rain abated, & we started coming away. Coming via the coastal area we collected three Sea Anenomes, & proceeded on only seeing all species mentioned again plus Sparrow (House) & a pair of Yellow Wagtails. Over the fields again we met J.S.
