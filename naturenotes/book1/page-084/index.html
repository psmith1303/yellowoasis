16.2.58
Peldon
With my sister Cynthia went along “Our” Marsh, & soon came upon a raft of calc. 200 Shelducks lovely in the sunlight. Plenty of Curlew in flocks across the Saltings, & Redshanks. we watched the Kestrel so good while quartering the marsh side, & then a Heron alighted. Two pairs of Mallard rose from the ditch a few feet from us. I saw one Hare. On our way back we came across a good unspoiled specimen of a ♂ fox, probably drowned. There were quite a few Skylarks seen today.
17.2.58
The Rooks are inspecting their old nests in St Martins church yard where there were 5 nests in 1957.
18.2.58
Mr Owen accepted the fox which was obtained from Peldon marsh, now in the N.H. Museum.
