Sunday March 10th 1957. Abberton Res, & District.

Observed Great Crested Grebes in summer plumage, & slight court ship display, numbers approx. 30. Pochard nesting near reeds edge, half dozen or more with numerous Tufted Duck (50 APROX) & Mallard. Heron’s not seen today. One Cormorant observed, took off and flew over water typical fast wing beats, white patch noticeable thigh, & cheek. Coots numerous, & B. H. Gull, & Herring Gulls, & Lesser B. B. Gulls. Of Herring Gulls saw field of maybe 200 birds settled, with Rooks, & did not notice any B. H. Gulls, of these species, with them. For the last three weeks to a month have had black head dress. Many Lapwings seen on adjoining fields of the reservoir in company of Gull, see whilst travelling. Rooks, & Jackdaw seen quite numerous.
