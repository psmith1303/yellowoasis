12&13.6.57
Colchester

On site of work discovered by watching adult birds, the nest of a Pied Wagtail. It was situated under the ridge tiles of an old barn which is unused. Upon ascending to it, Russell Leavett found there were four young, nearly fully fledged. Both parents continued feeding throughout the day, although very perterbed when close watching.

Some two hundred yards from this sight of old peoples bungalows Old Heath Road, is a reed covered pond, near which I saw parties of Greenfinches, & Goldfinches, & a pair of Bullfinches. A Warbler, or White throat was seen but not properly indentified. Also Swallows, & Swifts & the house martins near the pond, & a troupe of Blue Tits, & young.
