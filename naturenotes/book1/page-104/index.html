1.6.58
Aldburgh, Suffolk

The weather was cloudy at first but the sun shone after two-oclock for the rest of the day.

A break in the journey for a drink of tea along the roadside gave me time to catch an Orangtip which was damaged slightly, & released, there were several flying. Cranesbill, & vetches were plentiful here.

We stopped for lunch on a heath three miles from Aldburgh, & I collected a Latticed Heath Moth, & a couple of

In Aldburgh, & along coast road was Bladder campion in abundance. An avocet was seen flying in the direction of Orfford. I found a dead G.C.Grebe, & kept the head for a skull, it seemed the victim of oil pollution. An immature Goldfinch was seen on the heath, & plentiful Skylarks. Not many other moths or Butterflies were flying although the sun was brilliant, & warm.
