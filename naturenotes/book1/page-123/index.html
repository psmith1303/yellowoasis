Records of natural history. Page 123

…a Willow Warbler. Many Swallows were in evidence, & by the cliffs House, & Sand martins. Here we saw a party of 29 Eider ducks, one in whitish plumage. Near the car park we saw a strange bird with sparrows, & this looked very much a hybrid Sparrow X Greenfinch. Botanically we found much Ragwort & Sea Aster (good blooms) & another for identification later. August Recent “sugaring” for Moths particularly in Serpantine Walk have produced a few Red Underwring. Many Square Spot Rustic, & Mouse Moth & “George” Yellow Underwing. Copper Underwing, & angle shades.
