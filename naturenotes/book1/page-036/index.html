22.5.57
…Shoveller Duck, & tufted, & the Turnstone. Gargeney, & Mallard, & Lapwing. On Birch park lake saw seven Mallard ducklings, & Duck, & Drake in reeds. Arrived home 7.20.
22.5.57 :
6.10 - 8.30
Climbed tree of bats nest, & disturbed them, causing all of them to fly out - in one direction - of the first one to leave. Could not count how many but maybe about 30 all appearing the same size. The colour was dark brown, & chestnut. Wing span about 8 inches. [Assumed by me to be NOCTULES OR GREAT BAT.] They did not come back. There was an awful odour at the holes - unbearable. After this we, - Russell, & I walked in direction which Bats few did not see them. Collected some very nice Ferns, then heard a drumming Woodpecker, did not see it. Leaving the wood we observed a pair of Marsh Tits feeding young at nest hole, & they were very interesting to watch. it was a lovely warm sunny evening.
