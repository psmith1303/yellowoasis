… After going as far as we had time for we turned around, & started back. Up until then we hadn’t seen the Avocets we’d hoped to see, but Cynthia then spotted three on the opposite bank, on Havergate Isle, with two Oystercatchers. Lovely birds the all looked together. In the fresh water by the sea wall were the young ducklings of the Shelduck, who flew around very anxiously. We couldn’t count them for they dived, & stayed submerged for a good while, & surfacing like corks for a second, & dive with a splash! We could look right a the birds, & hardly see them on the water, the camouflage was excellent. Had lovely close-up view of the Oystercatcher from about 20 yds. A Heron was seen about three times during the day, & once it alighted for a brief minute.

On the road home was confronted with young Pheasants crossing the road. They looked every bit like the little Bustard, with the characteristic Bustard…
