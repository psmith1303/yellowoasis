27.4.57
Abberton

Took walk through shooting grounds. The sun was warm, & it was a lovely evening.

The first bird I saw was a Magpie, & then I heard the ah! ah! ah! Of the Green woodpecker, & observed it through my glasses hopping down a tree trunk, the yellow rump was very clear at about two hundred yards on the tree. Had lovely view of a pair of Bullfinches lovely birds, very shy & timid. The birdsong was in terrific volume, & the Nightingales were very prominent. I saw one but there seemed hundreds everywhere. Saw a flock of Pigeons, also had view of Jay from about 8ft, then a little owl flew past. As I came away saw Snipe fly up from marshy ground. A lovely sight was a pair of Pheasants glide down the woodland slope. Heard Cuckoo again. [Note tucked in listing eggs for sale.]
