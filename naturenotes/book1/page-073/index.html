8.11.57
Walton On Naze

With members, & juniors of the C.N.H.S. visited the Naze on a live ornithology lesson, a field follow up of the “Bird” courses.

The weather was bright after a heavy downpour earlier, & at 9.30 we were away with a west wind, seven of us in Neal’s car.

The first walk around the shore only revealed about five Sanderlings, & from then not much at all before dinner except Goldfinches, 1 Bullfinch, & Greenfinches. After lunch we made another round of the Naze, & went far along the salting shore to a “Watchers Hut”. We got quite excited to see about twelve Eider Ducks male & female & approx: 30 Long-tailed ducks, my first of these species, after observing these for a while we continued along to see many more Sanderlings, Ringed Plovers, Turnstone, Grey Plovers, & Redshanks. c 1000 in all. Further along we came accross a partly buried Swan (Mute) which was a skeleton, & D. Barton had a perfect skull.
