23.11.58
Walton / Naze

…to a large sheet of water (where new sea wall operations were going on) & I saw a small bird dive, which I presumed to be a Little Grebe, I never saw it again properly, as we carried on.

In the botanical line was a few heads of Ragworts, & as many daisies. Tamarisks still grows down there. Other common birds seen on our return were a ♂ Chaffinch & approx. 200 Lapwings, Robins, Thrush & blackbirds were seen along eroded sea cliff.

The following are a list of species seen:-

3+7app R.B. Margansers. 7 Brent Geese.

4 Eider ♂♂♂♀. 2 Grey Plover. 1 Purple Sandpiper.

Sanderling (flocks). Turnstone (flocks).

Ringed Plover (flocks). Dunlin (flocks).

Oyster Catchers (large parties). Shelduck (many in flight). 5 Common Scoter. Dunlins (parties).

G.B.B. Gulls (ca 50+ roosting). Common Gull,

Herring, & B.H. Gulls present throughout day. Rock Pipit (several). Red shank (single & pairs here & there). 1 G.C. Grebe. &

Curlew (ca 12 seen in flight frequent intervals)

Chaffinch. Robin. Thrush. Blackbird. Lapwing. Starling. & Little Grebe.
