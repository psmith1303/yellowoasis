…of sight. At this stage we were keeping a keen eye open for Grey Carpet moths quite a rare moth definately confined to the immediate area. We eventually came to a field where Vipers Bugloss was more dominant than any thing else the field was supposed to be all Lucerne. Here we captured the first moth per J.F. then I captured one, & after that many more came up, & we took our wants, among them ♀ which J.F. hopes to breed. The food plant is the Flixweed which was abundant, so we had a good quantity of that, & Bugloss which I hope to transplant in Roman River area.

During this exciting period we saw ♂ Wheatear taking food to its young, which we saw nearby us, its chacking note we recorded, but not too successful. On tape we did get good impressions of the Lapwing, & Green Woodpecker.
