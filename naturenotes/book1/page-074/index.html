Records of natural history. Page 074

…stopped for a while, & watched G.B.B. Gulls in fair numbers. A Curlew nearly settled fairly close but continued on the move.

Quite a good hut, built by local watchers, certainly keeps the wind off. A great moment came when we spotted 16 Brent Geese, again my first of these species. After a while longer we started back, & saw 1 lone Sanderling. Party or “Rafts” of Mallard were offshore. Passed a boy Watcher? going towards the hut. Did not see the Eiders, or long tails on return, but a Diver of some species flew towards the car park. Many Lapwings on fields coming, & going.

An enjoyable day, fairly cold. We arrived home about 4.30.
