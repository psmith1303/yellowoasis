“On Holiday in Cornwall.

6-13.7.57
The weather for the most part was wet, & quite cool for July. But two days were very sunny, & warm.

Some very nice wild flower collected were Southern Marsh Orchid. Ragged Robin. Common Broomrape. WALL STONEWORT [written above struck out entry]. Sea Aster (Lands End). Bell Heather, Bladder campion. Purple Loosestrife.

Birds were not very abundant, & rather disappointing. But seen were Herring Gull. B.H. Gull. L.B.B.G. Cormorants colony (lands End). Shearwater. Oyster Catcher in flight. & the Stonechat. Greenfinches. Rock Pipit. While collecting wildflower on boggy land nearly trod upon an Adder which slipped away rather hurriedly. Flowers brighter in colour.
