[Tucked in, two items: Order form from C. H. Gowland, Naturalist, of “Tadorna,” 243 Pensby Road, Barnston, Wirral. Season 1947. Listing of eggs for sale and oologist’s supplies. Also Colchester Natural History Society First Annual Reported dated 1873.]

… Collected Heather, & Viper Bugloss for exhibition. Had very enjoyable day out, arrived home 8-30. P.S.Yellow hammer seen in pines.

Sunday 14.9.57

Out collecting specimens for society exhibition down Roman River area.

While collecting Reed made saw a Kingfisher flying down the river in direction of Bounstead rd, first Dive seen for about twelve years down this river. Green Woodpecker seen flying from ground. Saw a nice specimen of Dragon fly green in colour, & large. Observed a lovely Fritillary butterfly.
