Records of natural history. Page 118

…were taken, as was the caterpillar of the Goat moth, & Burnet moths. A swarm of bees were discovered & left alone. A couple of leverets were disturbed, & bolted away. A ♀ Partridge was disturbed near the young. One Lingard was caught, & a few more seen. One Toad was seen, & all gathered round this now uncommon creature.

Photographs of plants were taken by Mrs Long, Mr Mead, & Mr Kent. A nice discovery in the Beech woods was Broad-leaved Hellorborine which was well out being a creamy outside colour, with orangish lip.

On the wayside coming, Mrs Long showed us Sulphur Clover, & here I found the Buff Ermine moth.
