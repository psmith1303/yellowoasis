…of six feet, two at a higher level near the ceiling, one dead young was found in the water. The nests were very well constructed and quite deep, mounted on posts affixed to the wall. The nearest building (at a farm) are about a mile away from this site, on the north side which is the side used by the Swallows, out of the slits across the marsh.

On returning plenty of Greenfinches were sunning themselves, with Yellowhammers. Parties of Blue-tits were busy. We heard the Corn Bunting & there we many Skylarks about appearing very light on underparts. Two or three (Voles or Mice) dashed under cover up the sea-wall. At this point two Curlews flew further on, & the the sight of the day was seen when about c.a. fifty Curlews took wing, & calling passed a Heron in flight.

Left about 10am.

Russell saw a Heron (Ardea Cinerea) at Marriages mill, on opposite side of bridge.
