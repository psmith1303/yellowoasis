26.5.57
'The Brec' - Norfolk'
11.50

Went to Norfolk “Brec” with Mr J. Firmin, & Co.

The Brec is a large piece of untouched common land of poor soil, & flint pieces with mosses, & lichens, & sparse tough grass.

On arrival we spotted by a telegraph pole a Pheasant & young chics, which flew as we approached within three feet. The next species we spotted was my first sight of a Wheatear which in my opinion is a lovely bird these were then seen much more of in pairs. Almost immediately my first sight of a Stone Curlew was fulfilled, & this flew away rapidly. Having a good walk around the Brec stalking & watching Curlews, & Wheatears.
