On this cold snowy-slushy day, C.W. & myself visited the Reservoir which was frozen over in most places.

There were Mallard, Pochard & Widgeon & Teal in the tufts of grass, & a few in the water round the edges. I noticed through C.W.s new Solar Ross 9x35 a dead duck which turned out to be a duck Teal 1st year Pochard probably had been shot, as empty cartridges were seen on the causeway. Other birds (Gulls) had taken the entrails. ♀ NO SPECULUM. White underwing feathers. Tail, rump, head, & shoulder mantle mottled. Darky brown. Middle of back light brown finely marble, or mottled grey. Dark brown bill. Feet grey-white, black webs.

[Tucked in on opposite Page: Ink and watercolour drawing - head of 1st year Pochard. ♀ natural size and colour from dead specimen Abberton Res. Sun 26-1-58]

On going round to the other causeway were many duck were roosting in the grass tufts. Mallard were quite close in, Widgeon, Teal, & Common, & B.H. Gulls, & 1 G.B.B.G. A Heron was present all the time of our stay. Waders were represented by Turnstones & a snipe. A couple of Pied Wagtails were along the causeway, & around the traps were Greenfinches, & Chaffinches. One solitary Cormorant flew over. Coot, & Moorhen were feeding in the fields nearby. Noticed one dead Swan ice-bound. Leaving this side…
