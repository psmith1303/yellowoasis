21.7.57
Cherry Tree Area to Abberton

With Cince, & Wilfred Woolsey, rambled accross field, & downs in Cherry tree area, first encountering a wood which we went through. noticed a few Hazel nuts, & the Woodrush. Pigeons, & Magpies also few out.

Out of the wood, & in more open land came accross quite a lot of Common Centuary which looked very nice, plenty of vetch, & trefoil about, & further out in the slopes plenty of wild Scabious. We came accross about six big clumps of Musk Mallow altogether, & this plant wold make a nice decoration. The Weather was warm, & sunny, & changing windy in spasms, & changing to Thunderstorms, this weather did not suite Butterflies, & Moths very well. In the bright spells plenty of Meadow browns appeared one Common Blue, & Common Skippers. The Magpie Moth I collected one of. The six-spot Burnet was in plenty feeding on the Scabious.

At this stage just before lunch…
