6-4-57
Ford Street to Colchester
10AM

Embarked upon ramble with other members of the C.N.H.S. including my sister Cince, from Fordstreet to Colchester via route along river. The weather was quite cold, with a fair Noreaster wind, & patches of warm sunshine.

No extra special birds were seen, but among the species seen we indentified. Carrion Crow, & nests abundant. Rooks & nest. Magpies, & nests plentiful. Jays. Blackbirds. Robins. Missel thrushs. Song-thrush. House, & tree sparrows. 2 Kestrels flying. Pied Wagtails. 2 Drake Mallards, & 1 Duck with Wrens, & Moorhends. 2 true Mallard, flying.

The Green woodpecker was not seen, but heard. Ring, & stock-doves. Immature Gulls. (Common). 1 Black-headed Gull. Heard Chiff-Chaff. Saw Cock, & hen pheasant. 2 Common Partridges. & 1 Little-Owl. & Jackdaws. The nest of a Blackbird was found with four, heavily marked eggs.
