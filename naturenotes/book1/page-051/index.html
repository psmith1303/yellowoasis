The nature notebooks of Keith Woolsey

Records of natural history. Page 051

…we saw Greenfinches. Goldfinches & Yellowhammer. Chaffinch. Two Greenwoodpecker on same tree, & more Magpies.

After lunch continued to the river side, & collected Great reed Mace. Teasel, & W. Plantain as well as water snails.

The Cinnabar moth larva was plentiful on Ragwort.

Under a hedge of Sloe found a Male, & female Brown-tail moth which I secured as specimens, & have since had a laying of eggs. Came accross a wild Cherry-plum tree, & sampled the sweet fruit.

In a water filled Sand pit caught a Dragon fly - blue in colour, & resting on Dock seed head. On way home found a chrysallis of the Six-spot-burnet which I took, & hatched out to prove what the specimen was, the Chrysallis was attached to a grass stem, & appeared the be a brown-tissue paper texture, & very tough. [Drawing of chrysalis in margin]

P.S. Disturbed five wild Ducks on river.
