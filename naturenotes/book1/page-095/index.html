27.4.58
Lexden area of Park land

Although quite sunny, a very fresh wind was blowing from the west when J.F. & myself continued our search for the Wryneck (Junx Torquilla). During recent early mornings of the 20th & 25th we have heard the call note a Qe! Qe! Qe! which carried a long distance, but unable to pinpoint or see the bird.

Today we had a whole morning at it from 6.30 but still only calls heard. However we were rewarded with the excellent views afforded us by a lovely ♀ Pied Flycatcher in the grounds of Lexden Park by the Lake, later in the morning Mr J. Rammage also saw the bird. Even further were our “early rising” rewards by seeing the Lesser Whitethroat, & Lesser spotted Woodpecker, followed by lovely views of Nuthatches, & Bull-finches. Even Treecreepers came very close.
