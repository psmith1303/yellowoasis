17.2.58
Colchester

On the 7th/2/58 J Sparkes brought along a Spoonbill which was shot in Suffolk about a week previous. An immature bird (black wing tips). Mr Crawshaw proceeded to skin this bird, & lecture.

Earlier this week J Sparkes brought along a Smew which also had been shot on the Abberton Resavoir. This was a lovely adult ♂ bird which J.S. skinned & handed over to Mr Owen of the N. H. Museum. Other specimens J.S. gave were a Redwing?, ♀ Bullfinch, & Chaffinch ♂. During this week 5 Common Cranes were seen in the area of Waldeningfield Suffolk seen 10.2.58, & up to this date of entry. Mr J. Firmin reports one bird had been shot. (Three cases of birds indiscriminately shot, & ignorance) “Didn’t know what it was so I shot, are to familiar even now. The birds are being watched by the R.S.P.B.
