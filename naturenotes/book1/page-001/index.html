1945
Colchester, T. Arcy. Reservoir Hythe Wivenhoe
10/3/46

Got my first egg of the season, which beats Lawton and Gauls record. It was a Tawny Owls, from the tree in our garden. When jumping down I cracked it severely, and it is useless in my collection.

On the same day, when I was with Gaul, we got ten more Rooks, from Whitehall Rookery, and one in the afternoon from near the Reservoir Road, T, Darcy.

28/3/46

One Thrush’s egg, from the Cemetery near the Chapel. This was broke when drilling, and another the same.

31/4/46

35 Rooks eggs from Resavoir Rookery which were easy trees, then in the afternoon we got 25 more Rooks eggs from Wivenhoe park Rookery, which we supposed deserted. These eggs we got in clutches. That evening I got one Thrush’s egg, from Down the Wood in the garden, drilled, and blown successfully.
