23.3.58
Walton-on-Naze

On this very cold sunny day visited the Naze with, C. Woolsey, & R. Leavett. The wind was very strong, & from the South-east, & changing to east.

We arrived at approx 11.30, & walked down the cliff, & along the beach. The very first bird we saw was a ♀ Eider, which was apparently injured, & an immature bird, which was resting on the beach, taking to the water as soon as we approached very near. We carried on further & noticed the usual B.H. Gulls in full summer plumage. Three Sanderlings were seen next, & watched as they ran around “as on skates”.

We left the beach further on after seeing two more Sanderling, & a Grey Plover, & continued across the fields of Gorse, & Briars hoping to see some early migrants. We saw none.

Nearing the sea wall again we next saw Redshanks, & a pair of very brilliant Greenfinches. Fairly common were Skylarks.

C.W. noticed a Weasel slide into a bank…
