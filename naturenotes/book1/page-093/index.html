30.3.58
Abberton, Reservoir

With C.W. went to resavoir to see G.C. Grebes courtship display (raised up in water, & each bird facing, & touching mandibles). Two or six Reed Buntings were seen, & pointed out to R.L. when was met on causeway, & saw a Yellow Wagtail on the Layer causeway. A Heron was seen in the bordering grasses & rush. A few Pochard were in a flock, & plenty of Coots. Two Grebes were seen in flight at this stage, & seemed quite large. 52 Shelducks in fields.

On other causeway was one Cormorant, & plenty more pairs of grebes. One Herring gull was in a party of Black headed Gulls. On return home collected some Primroses, & “Pussy Willow”. Whilst collecting primroses disturbed a few pairs of Mallard from the flooded swamps. Further into Abberton on the Mersea road discerned the very nice Lesser periwinkle in quite good quantities.
