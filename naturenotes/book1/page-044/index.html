23.6.57
Royston Heath. Herts.

The weather was sunny, & warm with a slight breeze, & cumulus clouds, after a very pleasant journey we arrived about eleven. After selecting a spot where we put our gear we split up into two parties, & one took the woods after bird, & the other the hill slopes are Butterflies, Moths & plants.

Being with the wood party we plodded through high growths of Nettles, & didn’t see very much. We came across an unusual nest supposingly of a Thrush but being composed of twigs, & moss, lined in the usual manner, situated on the side of an old dead Fir tree, we took the nest as a specimen. Further through the wood we sat awhile in a “ride”, & observed a “Tawny Owl” being mobbed by other birds including Blackbirds, & Thrushes. The Wood Warbler was heard only.
