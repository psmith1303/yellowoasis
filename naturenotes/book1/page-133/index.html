14.12.58
Lexden Park, & 'Scarletts'

Took Russell Leavett to see the Bramblings in Lexden park. At first we were not very lucky - only seeing one ♀. However we waited patiently, & after a time moved slowly to a hill-top, near some big beeches, & here we watched many of these birds, which turned over leaf after leaf, & then would fly up to perch in the branches, before dipping down once again, & showing a nice white rump. Whilst watching these a flutey note attracted our attention overhead, & we saw a Nuthatch climbing around, he didn’t stop long & flew off quickly. Many of the tit family were in the beech trees as well as the Chaffinches. We saw many Greenfinches when we came into the park by the lake & Yew trees, J.F. & I saw them in the same spot the previous Sunday.
