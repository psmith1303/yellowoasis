…& returning to the first causeway we saw a Swan fly over, & this probably was a Bewicks swan, as this seemed rather different, & alarmed to be a Mute.

Leaving the Resavoir, & going to our woodland by “our” stream. As soon as we parked the motor cycle near a little stream, a pair of Duck flew up not recognised. Not much to be seen round here until in the wood a Woodcock + 1 long tailed tit was flushed, & then a pheasant. Continued, & had our lunch in a barn, & continued our walk back along the river, a duck half-flew, & swam down stream apparently injured it looked like a female POCHARD, a pair of duck flew up further along didn’t recognise them. A Green Woodpecker flew into a tree, & provoke an attack & a tit which settled on the same limb, they both flew off. Quite a few Blackbirds about. Just as we were leaving a Greater Spotted Woodpecker flew into some Willow nearby. From this area we carried on to Peldon having a quick glance over our marsh.
