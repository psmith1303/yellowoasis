…plentiful, so were Ringed Plover, & Reeves, & Dunlin two more firsts actually recognised by Neal Syer, & Mr Snoxall.

Fairly well round by this time, & Waders were in plenty. Towards the first causeway I had the very best view of the Kingfisher I’ve ever had, & later saw another again on a wire-trap on the other causeway, & at the first again the Little Ringed Plover. 3. Saw the most Herons I’ve ever seen as many as fifteen nearly altogether. It was hoped to see a Spoonbill which was seen previously seen - but we didn’t have any luck.

At Layer-Breton causeway four Snipe got up, & flew accross. I noticed a few G.C. Grebes in winter plumage, but an absence of Tufted Duck. About four Cormorants were seen flying over the water. 29 Species. Starlings were around quite a bit. We arrived back at 10-00. P.S.One Curlew I saw as it took flight.
