…was the fact more than one nest was in there. As many as a dozen Swallow were in, and out, all the time we observed then. Coming back along the estuary we saw two Cormorants in flight & more Curlews, & Redshanks of which we had good views. Again we saw the bird we could not recognise maybe a M. Harrier? tried for good recognition but soon was out of sight along the hedge of a cornfield. A few butterflies along the sea wall were large, & small Meadow Brown. Common blue. Frittilary -?- & Painted lady looking very beautiful.

Rabbits were seen, & a vole, further near the roadside heard a shrew squeaking in long grass.

After leaving the saltings, we cont’d, to the reservoir were we saw. Ringed Plover. Common (Ducks). Sandpiper, & Little Terns. Approx 200 swan were in the area of Layer-de-la-Haye church end.
