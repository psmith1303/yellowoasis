Records of natural history. Page 096

…sparrow’s, a wren, & numerous tits, Great & blue. Starlings nesting. Pigeons, & rooks & Jackdaws. Five Stock Doves took wing from near Lexden church. Coming from this area into Church Land we observed the Nuthatches again, & saw, & heard the ♂ Blackcap. We entered the park again to see the Tree-creepers, & numerous Swallows, & the Nuthatch again, when Mr Ramage, & son arrived. We immediately showed him the Pied Flycatchers which we did not see when entering the park the second time. We strolled around searching for L.S. Woodpeckers no success, but had wonderful views of two Red Squirrels leaping from tree to tree, & climbing very fast. Upon leaving here we can down to the lake where J.R. noticed a House martin. The Pied Flycatcher was still there, & numerous Chaffinches. Greenfinches were in the Yew trees.
