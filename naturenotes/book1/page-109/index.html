…nests in various positions all round the interior. The nests’ contained as follows. 2 with four eggs 2 with five eggs, & the last two, close to one another, contained four well fledged young. Two additional nests were found & presumed to be last year nests. In one recess, or gun-slit, with an earth flooring was a slight scrape & lining with five empty last year eggs.

During the entry of the pill-box all the swallows flew out, to quick to count, & these flew around over a field about half a mile away; remembering this, & the young birds we soon made off after I discovered mammal bone remains in the water, as well as the skull of a bird looking like a gulls, & one dead adult Swallow of 1958 which showed all the feathers, & colour very well.

On our way back we found a tent of Ground-lackey’s which I took…
