23.11.58
Walton / Naze

Set off by train for the naze at about 10 o’clock, accompanied by R.L, & Alan Wood.

Upon arrival we walked along promenade observing a few Herring Gulls. It was some way along the sandy beach before we spotted our first wader on this rather cold overcast day. Among the party were Sanderling, & Ringed plover, & Turnstone, not much further along were a few more, with Herring Gulls & G.B.B. Gull in the water just offshore. At this point we also saw three Brent Geese in flight.

Going around the sea wall where a lot of erosion has taken place we noticed a G.C. Grebe flying & settled about 50yds out on the water. Another bird watched pointed out a Purple Sandpiper among a party of Dunlin’s, & Ring Plover, & we had fairish views of this dark headed Sandpiper. Four more Brent geese in flight & 3 R.B. Mergansers a first for my records. Along this particular piece of beach we saw many waders, & a group of Eiders, the drakes were in adult plumage. 2 Grey Plover at this stage.
