Records of natural history. Page 055

…flying around now. We saw about five Shelduck feeding, & four Tufted swimming in the water of mid-channel. Observed a small party of Gulls flying & moving along, & continuing this movement again, this was caused by a Cormorant which was swimming, & diving. Later during the day we saw a pair flying about, & also singly. A pair of Kestrel were quatering the sea wall & marsh, we had lovely views of these all day. At dinner time Russell disturbed our first Heron, & then later we saw four, & three together, singly they were active nearly all day, here, & there. More Snipes flushed with Lapwings, along ditch side. A quite unusual nesting sight for swallows was discovered in a disused “Pillar box” along sea wall; this was filled by water to two feet deep approx., & then the birds came in at the gun slits, strange…
