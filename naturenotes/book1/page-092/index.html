…just as R.L. & I watched a Hare go over the seal wall. As we came to the sea side of the wall we noticed large parties of Sanderling, with Grey-plover. Turnstones, & Redshanks, & Dunlin. A black duck seen in the sea seemed very much like a Velvet skoter?. Two ducks flew up from the saltings but we were unable to recognise them ‘out of light’. We had an enjoyable lunch watching Redshanks Ringed plovers, & another &9792; Eider, L.B.B. Gulls few over & the B.H. were still around. Whilst watching these birds R.L. noticed a dead bird which was a G.B.B. Gull. I obtained a fine head. The return journey was slack seeing only Turnstones & Sanderlings. As we came to the cliffs again R.L. picked up some Coltsfoot which was common where small trickles of water cascaded down & we found more further on in plenty.
