…watching more waders, & gulls, & several Rock pipits.

After lunch we continued along this beach further than I’d ever been before, & here I found a rotted corpse of an Eider ♀, the skull was in good condition, so I kept it for my collection. The tide was well out by now, & revealed a large firm sand bank, upon which were many birds including 50+ G.B.B. Gulls & many Oyster catchers which were piping away merrily, we spotted five dark coloured duck-looking birds, which after a little ponderance we identified as common Scoters, which took wing as we approached a little nearer. Approx; seven more R.B. Mergansers were seen in flight towards the west, & then a very large flock of Shelduck came over. On this sand-bank were small hillocks of Clams or Mussels, & many more marine shellfish.
