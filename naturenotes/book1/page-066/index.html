20.10.57
Peldon Marsh
7.0am

With Russell Leavett set out over the sea wall on a frosty misty morning, which soon cleared, & became sunny, & warm, with only a stiffish breeze off the incoming water.

Directly over the sea wall through the mist we saw a few ducks feeding unable to indentify but probably Mallard, Teal & Widgeon. As soon as these took wing we saw, & heard a few Redshanks. This early morning expedition was intended specially to investigate more fully the nesting Swallows (Hirundo Rustica) in the Pillbox in the sea Wall.

However, before reaching our objective we saw, & heard the lovely Curlews (Numenius arquata) & saw flocks of Redshank, & gulls.
