10.6.57
Abberton

Spent afternoon in field of Briars and Hawthorn’s near stream by Abberton Manor.

First birds seen were Swifts, & then the Whitethroats. It was wet everywhere, & showery, & cold. Walking towards the stream down the sloping field saw a Linnet perched high, & then Yellowhammers. Great & Blue-Tits were in the higher border hedge. Hoped to record some Red Backed Shrikes but no luck, or signs of them anywhere.

Saw a bird which I could not accurately recognise but looked like Cirl Bunting? or Reed Bunting? & possibly a Stone-Chat, disappointed at not being able to tell. Nothing down by river, but not far off under briar was a “set’ of a Fox? Badger? with one dropping, chicken feather, & fish scales around no smells. [Drawing of fish scale actual size in margin.]

Coming away had lovely views of a long-tailed Tit, then a Blackcap? or Marsh Tit.
