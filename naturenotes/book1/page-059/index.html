8.9.57
Norfolk Brec

On a day out with J. Firmin to Norfolk Brec to see the Stone Curlew - congregating. A lovely sunny day with a slight westerly wind, & quite warm.

Altogether in the flocks of S. Curlew which we followed around the ground we counted as many as thirty, there were possibly more but single birds here, & there confused the count. We had good views, & once stalked them up-wind & got quite close. Many Pheasants & Partridges were about every where & Hares. We only saw one Wheatear, an immature bird, very shy. The Woodlark was quite abundant & could be heard as ‘twiswrrt”, as well as see the short tail. Collected a few moulted S. Curlew feather for exhibition. During lunch time away from Brec saw Long-tailed-tits in Birch, & collected varieties of Lady birds as well as one unknown larvae, green in colour, red stripe alongside.
