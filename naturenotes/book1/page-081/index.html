The nature notebooks of Keith Woolsey

Records of natural history. Page 081

Previous page Next page

Noticed two gunners probably after Hares. Over the Sea-wall we disturbed a few Redshank, & quite a few Curlew. A couple of Shelduck flew over. We never ventured further so returned to the motor-cycle, in doing so a Green Woodpecker flew to a mole-hill and was seen scraping about. A formation of Cormorants nine in number came over, & one passed over separating from the rest. The last locality for the day was at Mersea, where the snow was quite bad, the tide was in, so the Brent geese were not feeding however we saw about fifty in flight wheel over very low toward Bradwell. Along the tide line we found first a Common Gull, & then a complete, & fresh Brent-Goose no apparent cause of death. On examining the crop it revealed a little Zostera & other marine shoots, & an unrecognised food, the bird was very thickly feathered & very meaty-dark of flesh. The bird was darkly plumed all over, & white under tails feathers, & rear under parts, the beak of both species were obtained.
