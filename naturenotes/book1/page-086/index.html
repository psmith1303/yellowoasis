2.3.58
Colchester

[Tucked in: 8” x 6” black and white photograph. On reverse: “Waxwing (Bombycilla Garrulus) Photographed by C.P.Rose @ Cambrai Rd. Colchester. March 1958.

Mr N Syer informed me on the 1.3.58 of the presence of Waxwings in Messines, & Cambrai Road. This morning (Sunday) I went along, & immediately saw one bird which flew further up the road, to where I saw a second bird. The trees there were on contained many berries, though the two trees I saw them feeding on were preffered to the others along further which were even more loaded with berries. I think the trees are probably black hornbeam.

The weather today has been very sunny, & a slight S.E. wind. Temp about 35-40°. The Waxwings are very tame, & indifferent to the closest observations (even without binoculars) of any human beings. The birds kept well about seven feet from the ground, & up to the highest part of the trees approx seventeen feet. A resident in a house nearby has noticed these birds since before…
