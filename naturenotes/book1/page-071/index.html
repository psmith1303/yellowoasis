1.12.57
Old Heath Colchester

[Tucked in: Cutting from [Essex County?] Standard Friday November 29 1957. “Birds dead, as if tar-dipped Oil pollution: £400 fine for Greek skipper £230 to Mersea U.D.C.”]

With Joe Firmin, & Miss C. Woolsey, visited the residence of Mr Duckworth where we looked around his garden, & woodland, also to fix six bird-nesting boxes, lettered from A to F.

During the look around the wood-land, we discovered two Chrysaliss of Moths, also an newly emerged Umber Moth? C. Rustic? both behind old bark of willow.

Whilst fixing the first nest box watch our only member of Certhiidae the Tree Creeper of which we had lovely views. Later on we had good views of a pair of Bullfinches. J. Firmin kept them near by calling them up to twenty feet.

Went to Mersea in the afternoon to see any effects of the recent oil pollution of which the skipper of the oil tanker was fined £400, & vessel impounded. Very glad to see no birds today were effected, & an absence of any oil in quantity.

A Cormorant or Cormorants were flying back, & forth. A G.B.B. Gull…
