6.10.57
Abberton Reservoir

With Neal at 5.30 went around the road at the resavoir. The sun was coming up, & made the sky red, & a blazing pathway of gold accross the water. The wind was North-westerly, & quite chilly. The Swan’s were there, & sighted nearly first, as were the huge rafts of Coots. The Little Grebe was seen where I observed it once before. Plenty of Duck about, unable to see them clearly as we faced the sun. On turning around, and taking the other side road we saw about 50 Shelduck. Further on Neal saw a Kingfisher gone when I looked. Magpies, Crows, Cormorants, & plenty of Gull were the, one or two G. B. Gulls. Not until we were around to the Wigborough bay did we see any waders, apart from very many Lapwings in display showing beautiful chestnut undertails colourings. The next wader was a Wood Sandpiper, then we saw the “little” Ringed Plover, & Dunlins, & more…
