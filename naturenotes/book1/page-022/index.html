23.3.57
7:55

On 15th March 1957 a new rookery was observed by me in St Martins churchyard West Stockwell Street Colchester, which can be said is the most centrally located Rookery in Colchester. Next nearest to town centre being in Lexden Rd by the C.R.G. School. The Rookery in the churchyard is in a Horse chestnut tree, there being three nests. There doesn’t appear to be any more pairs attempting to nest, although the birds flying around were counted and more than three pairs of Rooks were seen, this was in the evening around 6.30pm.

Droppings are quite few at present & no mess is apparent, although many twigs (dropped probably) were lying around.

By the traffic to, and fro from nests there doesn’t appear to be any incubation started as pairs at nests were both flying, & still building nests, date 23.3.57.

.57.
