Records from Windsor

The following are records from a letter sent to J. Firmin, from a game-keeper friend on an estate in Windsor Park, formerly assistant to Maj. Gen. Wainwright at Abberton Res.

During 1956-7-8 Golden oriole present & one ♂ singing well during 1956.

Wryneck. Two present 1956, one adult, one Juv.

1957

One Juvenile in Autumn.

1958

One on migration.

The Canada Goose is the most common Wildfowl in Windsor, & number kept down, because of being harmful. The Mandarin duck nests successfully & recurs in large numbers.

Among the Starling roost which B. Winchester entered, he estimated 500,000 roosting in 3 acres of 14 yr old scotch fir trees, & two Albinos were noted. When he came out of this plantation he was covered in droppings & appeared “whitewashed.”
