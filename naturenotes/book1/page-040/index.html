… the very first sight of the lovely “Avocet” of which we saw five, all in flight, call note not unlike a strong Redshank call rather more clear, & distant. The bird appears to me an arch shaped body, with tail, & legs drooped rather, & colour, & mandibles very clear, a rare, & lovely time.

On return journey noticed the Oystercatcher still around same spot. An owl was flying around in broad daylight, & it was bright & sunny, & warm, the bird to all indentifications appeared to be a Short-eared owl, it was very active for about two hours.

A single Ringed Plover was seen feeding, then further on I saw what appeared to be a Grey-Wagtail, but not definatley recognised, as well as a Rock-pipit, it fitted text book details pretty well, & habitat was suited very well.

On journey home saw pheasants, & chicks, & Partridges.
