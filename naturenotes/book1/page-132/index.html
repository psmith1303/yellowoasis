7.12.58
Lexden Park Colchester

On this very cold, foggy day with Joe Firmin, C. Rose, & J. Rammage in Lexden park watching Brambling my first view of these birds. It was hard to count how many there were but a rough estimate would be anything from a dozen to twenty. We all had an excellent view of a male which perched about fifteen feet from us, other bramblings were darting about, with a very noticeable white rump, differing from the Chaffinchs, with which the “brambles” were feeding, & not really easy to pick out from each other. A Tree Creeper was climbing a nearby tree, & Tits were flashing about, in the tree tops. Castle Road On a visit to my cousin @ 16 Castle road I saw a ♂ Lesser spotted Woodpecker, & watched it for quite five minutes, as it climbed about an ash tree.

SISKINS I am informed that the Siskins of 1957 @ Scarletts are back again from 30.11.58.
