Records of natural history. Page 103

…Early spotted orchid, & Saxicle. Many trefoils, & vetches, & other unidentified (as yet) species. At lunch I discovered three large Woodant nests. The amount of other insect life was enormous, & untold species. A very good day ended at about 9.30 from the early start at 6.28.

Identified species were as follows.

WOODRUFFE (GALUM ODORATUM)

SANICLE (SANICULA EUROPAEA)

COMMON MILKWORT (POLYGALA VULGARUS)

LOUSEWORT (PEDICULARIUS SYLVATICA)

HEATH SPOTTED ORCHID (DALYORCHIS MACULATA)

CHALK MILKWORT (POLYGALA CALCAREA)

30.5.58
Joe Firmin, & I went to Donyland Woods to collect larva of White Admiral butterflies off Honeysuckle no success. However I collected a Gold-tail larva, & probable (Merville du Jour). [Margin note: This larva emerged later as the Satelite moth.] On our way in we found the Bat roost I searched for previously this season. Mr Owen will be informed, & an effort to ring the Bats will be made.
