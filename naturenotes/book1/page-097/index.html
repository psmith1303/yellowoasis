3.5.58
Framsden, Suffolk

With a bus load of C.N.H.S. members & two cars, we visited the ancient meadow of the “Snakes-head Frittilary” (Frittillaria Mealeagris) at Framsden. After waiting for the leader Mr Blaxill, we proceeded to the meadow were we beheld a wonderful site of the lovely wild flowers, which are supposed to have been untouched since the middle ages.

Members were given permission to have one flower each, which most people did.

With the normal mauve coloured species were white coloured unspotted ones, and lovely double headed species of both colours.

The meadow contained Kingcups, & a few cowslips. (seeing plenty on the journey there). & Cuckoo-pint, & Buttercups. A most enjoyable afternoon. Also saw my first Turtle dove of ‘58.
