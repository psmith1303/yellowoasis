9.6.58
Peldon Marsh

With C.W. & R.L. we made an evening expedition over the mash-sea wall area at Peldon. On our way there we saw the normal birds such as Redshanks first, Skylarks, Meadow pipit. Shelduck’s, & young, eight to a dozen in number. & Curlews, & B.H. Gulls & Little & Common tern. 1 Heron. & a ♂ Whinchat, usual Corvidae. & Starlings in large flocks. The main idea was to check on the nesting swallows in the Pill box of the 20.10.57 (Ref back pages). The water inside was still as deep as it was in the first occasion, however I took off boots, & socks rolled up trousers, & waded in. The very first thing I saw was a dead Barn Owl floating in the water, it had been dead a week or more at least, the body was in a state of putrifaction & disintegrated when I hauled it outside. R.L. found 1 pellet in one of the gun-slits, presumably Barn owl.
