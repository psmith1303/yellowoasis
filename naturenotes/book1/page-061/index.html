22.9.57
Walton Naze

Went with J.S. & N.S. to see the migrant birds along coast line.

The first bird we saw there was a Wryneck, there were a few people about quite near but we had very good views. The wind in the morning had been in the east, but had changed to west, & North west during the evening. Not much movement but we saw about twelve Wheatears six Redstarts. One Meditteranean black headed Gull identified by the pearl back markings & slight larger size than Black-headed Gull (in winter plumage). One Pied flycatcher lovely view & Spotted Flycatcher. My first Common Scoter was seen diving in the sea, not a very clear view, also seen here were Turnstones - Redshanks - Ringed Plover. Oystercatchers. Mallard in flight. On return collected 2 or 3 doz “Dor beetles” in Cow Dung. Great. B. Black Common, & Herring Gulls were seen…
