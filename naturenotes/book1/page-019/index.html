20.1.57
SUNDAY

Went bird-watching across marshes as far as brightlingsea, from the Mersea Rd at Peldon.

Birds seen on this cold day, with grey clouds, included about c.a. 500 Shelduck, 50 Curlews, Redshanks, Wild ducks, 1 Great Crested Grebe, 1 Cormorant Comon, G.B.B. B.H. & Herring Gulls, 1 Snipe flushed in first field. Rooks, & Crows were plentiful. Skylarks, Blackbirds, Moorhen and Meadow pipits included the species.

A very enjoyable time, very muddy conditions, accompanied by my sister Cynthia. We found a shelduck eaten by presumably a fox, all intact except for guts. On return journey carcase had gone. Exceptionally pleasing was the call of the Redshanks, & curlews, & everlasting memory. The Cormorant was seen indulging in washing itself turning on its back kicking its feet, & showing itself repeating this performance several times. As dusk was falling & visibility was poor, we saw & heard Curlews and Redshank quite clearly the stillness echoing the delightful call across the saltings.
