15.6.58
Eriswell Brecklands

With J.F. & Family in Mr Firmin Snr’s car to Eriswell brecs, & Tuddenham for rare moth … with no success.

Had lunch at Tuddenham area & then on to brecs, on way stopped to survey fields headlands & captured a pretty pine feeding moth.

Also here was a very beautiful Wild Rock Rose of which we had specimens, & other specimens to be named later.

Dwarf growing Tufted vetch was first seen on the brecks, & plenty of Wild Thyme. Plenty of young Tits everywhere, & young pheasants. One pheasants nest or Partridge? contained 22 eggs (& 6 Pheasant eggs & 16 Partridge). Then hen Partridge was sitting tight, & we flushed her to see this uncommon occurrence (which has been known before).

Not far in the brecks we saw three Stone Curlew, which ran to our left over the brecks, & out…
