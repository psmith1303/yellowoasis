28.7.57
Orfford Ness

Visited the Orfford area with Cynthia, & Russell Leavett. The weather was quite cool, & overcast with rainy periods, changing winds.

Within the first hundred yards by the water way of the sea wall collected eggs of some moth, or butterfly, & unable to identify as yet. A large number of Pigeons were around the area of cultivated land. A snipe we disturbed kept flying, & “setting” alternately. The was high, & not such a lot of birds visible, but later observed B.H. Gulls, & Common Term. Curlews in various number i.e. twos, and fives, & a nice view of the Kestrel hawk hovering for longish periods along sea wall.

Not many butterflies about except two Common blue, a frittilary, & Cabbage whites, & Meadow browns, & common skippers. An unusual amount of queer coloured ladybirds about being of colour yellow ochre [?], & more finely spotted, & large.
