14.1.58
Wormingford

Whilst working @ “The Grange” Wormingford, the char-lady pointed out to R. Leavett, & my-self a perfect white albino blackbird, white as fresh fallen snow, quite large, & handsome a fit bird, which had a call being a variation of the normal blackbird’s single syllable call. I don’t know what sex it was but, the people of the house said it fought off other normal cock-birds, as far as they knew the legs, & bill were flesh coloured. This was observed when the bird had been feeding in the garden, over the two years it has been in the area.

I observed this bird in an apple orchard from about thirty or forty yards, it seemed very wary, & it flew quickly, rather like a Jay. Its unknown whether this bird breeds at all, but as a matter of interest the are no “piebald” birds among the many blackbirds about here.
