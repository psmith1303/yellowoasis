… I collected a few eggs which were useless to hatch, being over the nest side, & in water, or mud. Neal found a Redshanks nest with four of the beautiful marked eggs.

I took eight photographs of the young birds, & nests, but had no more for the Redshanks nest.

Other birds seen were Sandwich terns, Redshanks, Little Terns, & Curlew, & Oyster Catchers, & Dunlin.

All these we heard on returning home, also the Corn-bunting.

A good walk back to the car, & we had our “tea”, the time was 10.30. Home-coming in the car, we saw many Moths, & eventual time on arrival home was approx. 11.40. P.S. Saw my first Sandwich Terns flying over gullery.

During recent days I’ve collected a few Moth larva, & so far one has pupated (Drinker Moth). A Cream Spotted Tiger moth collected a West Mersea laid a good batch of eggs, which have now hatched, & okay.

Three larva were collected today (30.6.57)…
