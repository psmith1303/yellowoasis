9.5.58
Donyland, & Manwood

With R. Leavett went through Donyland woods. After parking cycles continued through main wood path, & then followed stream to a westerly direction. No signs, or noise of Bats in Ash tree of 1957. Numerous mosses, & liverworts were seen. Masses of Windflower still in bloom, & some with very mauve petal backs as the head drooped. not applicable to all flowers & this seems to occur in patches. We continued until we came to a spot where R.L. saw two, or three G.S. Woodpeckers, & sat on a pine log pile, & watched for a while only seeing a Marsh tit & Blue, & Great tits. Rooks, Magpies, & Jays & a Carrion Crow. We detoured round the wood then cut across. On examining an Oak tree in fresh leaf we found many insects with very long antenae (J.F. says they are Moths of the Micro family).
