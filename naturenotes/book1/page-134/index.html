…obligingly. Goldfinches were eating seeds from Alder Catkins, & at first we thought they were the Siskins, but the light was quite good, & we saw they were Gold finches. Magpies chacked away high over the Laundry area, & flew past many Gulls SPP on the pond. With two Coots, I noticed one Little Grebe, which partly flew to the other side of the pond, not far from the resident swans. I have had information about the grebes meeting there this year. We was rather surprised not to see any Bullfinches. Altogether we had an interesting morning although it was quite cold.

On the evening of Dec 5th I skinned, & prepared a Dunlin which RL and AW found whilst still alive at East Mersea, no apparent signs of death. Not very easy to skin as I found it tender, the first incision became enlarged to easy, so I had a difficulty sewing up.
