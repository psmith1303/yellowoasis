16.3.58
Abberton Reservoir

On this very cold afternoon went to the resavoir to the Layer Breton side first. There were very many birds seen everywhere today, although cold it was very bright, & sunny, with an easterly wind. Before going my wife, & I saw the Waxwing again at Cambrai Rd.

Two very interesting birds were seen on the causeway water edge. First seen were two Reed Buntings. & then I saw Yellow Wagtail. Two Herons were seen in the herbage. Cormorants, three in number were swimming close, & one on the concrete. G.B.Grebes were in Courtship dress, & were numerous. A few Tufted Ducks were seen, & many Gulls, & Lapwings. I picked up an immature B.H. Gull, apparently hit some wire or obstacle, it had a slight bloody head, & from mandibles.
