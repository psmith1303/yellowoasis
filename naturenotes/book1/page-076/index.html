29.12.57
'Scarletts'. Old Heath. Col.

All day operating “Mist nests” with R.L., N.S., J.R. & other younger naturalists.

These nets proved to be very successful, as within a few minutes of arrival birds were being caught. Two first in one net were a Dunnock & a Goldcrest which was an adult probably female, ringed, & released.

Next a Jay was caught then followed Blackbirds ♂, ♀, Blue-tits, & Great tits ♂, ♀, Bullfinches, a Tree-Creeper, & a Moorhen. Robin.

Watched a party of 20 or thirty Siskins among the Alder tree picking seeds from the small cones, a first of this species for my records. Parties of Bullfinches were about after, & single birds. Saw one Goldfinch, & a pair of Magpies.

A beetle I found under oak-bark turned out to my indentification to be a Carrion Beetle (Oeceoptoma thoracicum)x [label pasted onto page]. This beetle is about ½” long, & carries the elitra in two pieces, one under which the head contracts, the other over the rear portions. Its colour is a transparent oak brown on the edges, & darker over body. The head is very small, & retractable. A tail-like extension protrudes from the elitra. The antenna is rather straight, & serrated.
